# Korn Ferry — TA Trends 2026: Human–AI Power Couple

**Source:** https://www.kornferry.com/insights/featured-topics/talent-recruitment-articles/ai-in-recruitment-trends  
**Published:** October 28, 2025 (12th Annual Report)  
**Data:** 1,674 global talent leaders + 230 Korn Ferry talent experts surveyed  
**Period:** Survey conducted for 2026 outlook  
**Best for:** AI adoption in TA, autonomous agent trends, office mandate impact, critical skills priorities, TA strategic positioning

---

## Methodology

- **1,674** external global talent leaders (TA directors to CHROs)
- **230** Korn Ferry internal talent specialists and consultants
- Geography: 42% US, 26% EMEA, 20% APAC, 12% LATAM
- Company range: Startups to Fortune 500

---

## AI Adoption

| Metric | % |
|--------|---|
| TA leaders planning to use AI in 2026 | **84%** |
| Leaders planning to add autonomous AI agents to teams | **52%** |
| Companies planning to replace roles with AI | **43%** |
| Operations/back-office roles targeted for AI replacement | **58%** |
| Entry-level roles targeted for AI replacement | **37%** |
| Executives well-prepared for AI transition | **11%** |

---

## Skills Priorities (Top TA Hiring Priorities for 2026)

1. Critical thinking & problem-solving — **73% rank #1**
2. (2nd–4th not specified)
3. AI skills — ranked **5th**

- Only **22%** of companies planning leadership succession with AI readiness in mind

---

## Strategic Influence

- TA leaders using AI effectively: **85%** have C-suite influence
- TA leaders NOT using AI: **70%** have C-suite influence
- **83%** of TA leaders say they have C-suite influence overall
- **59%** still feel shut out of strategic business decisions

---

## Office Mandates

- **52%** say office mandates hinder recruiting
- **73%** say remote roles are easier to fill
- **20%** of companies now require full-time office attendance
- Full-time office requirements force a choice: pay premium or accept less-qualified candidates

---

## Six TA Trends for 2026

1. **Your next hire might not be human** — AI agents becoming autonomous team members with HR records
2. **Think first, ChatGPT later** — Critical thinking outranks AI skills in hiring priority
3. **Entry-level cuts = pipeline crisis tomorrow** — Eliminating early roles undermines future leadership
4. **Buying without buy-in** — Tech deployed without leadership readiness
5. **TA needs a bigger seat at the table** — TA evolving from ops to strategic advisor
6. **Office mandates = major TA headache** — Rigid return-to-office creates recruiting disadvantage

---

## Key Quote

> "Talent acquisition is about people—and human intelligence will always be the differentiator."

---

## Key Conclusions

- AI adoption is near-universal in TA planning (84%) but execution readiness is low (11% of execs feel prepared)
- Autonomous AI agents are moving from concept to practice — 52% plan to add them in 2026
- Critical thinking beats AI skills as the #1 hiring priority — organizations want humans who can govern AI
- Eliminating entry-level roles is a short-term cost save with long-term leadership pipeline risk
- Office mandates are a structural recruiting disadvantage that TA leaders are actively pushing back on
