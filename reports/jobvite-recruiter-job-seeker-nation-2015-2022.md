# Jobvite Recruiter Nation & Job Seeker Nation — Annual Series 2015–2022

## 2015 Recruiter Nation Survey

**Sample:** 1,400+ U.S. recruiting professionals (September 2015)

### Key Statistics
- **78% report finding best candidates through referrals** (up from 60% in 2014)
- **56% of recruiters cite lack of available skilled talent** as a key stumbling block
- 95% anticipate equal or increased competition for talent over the next year

### Social Media in Recruiting
- Only **4% of recruiters not using social media** in recruiting
- 87% use LinkedIn; 55% use Facebook; 47% use Twitter

### Candidate Quality Assessment Criteria
- Culture fit: 88%
- Previous job experience: 87%
- GPA: 57% say unimportant
- Cover letters: 63% say unimportant
- Face-to-face interviews: 96% still rely on them
- Resumes: 93% rely on them

## 2015 Job Seeker Nation Study

### Key Statistics
- **45% of job seekers used mobile devices to search for jobs daily**
- Social media increasingly important for job discovery

---

## 2016 Recruiter Nation Survey

**Sample:** 1,600+ recruiting and HR professionals

### Key Statistics
- **95% expect recruiting to be as or more challenging in 2017**
- **69% report their company's hiring has increased** in the past year
- 33% anticipate filling 100+ positions in the next year (up from 26%)

### Social Media & Mobile
- 87% use LinkedIn; 55% use Facebook; 43% leverage Facebook in recruiting
- Only **45% of recruiters' company career sites support mobile**

### Social Media Red Flags (Candidates)
- Typos/poor spelling: 72% view negatively
- Oversharing: 60% turned off
- Photos of alcohol consumption: 47% negative

### Top Candidate Qualities
- Cultural fit: 83%
- Enthusiasm: 78%
- Conversational skills: 73%

---

## 2016 Job Seeker Nation Study

### Key Statistics
- **74% of employees are open to a new job** despite many being satisfied in current role
- 39% say it's harder to find a job than the year before; 44% are optimistic
- 59% used social media to research company culture of organizations they were interested in
- 67% of those who used social media in job search used Facebook (vs. 35% Twitter)

## 2017 Job Seeker Nation Study (May 2017)

### Key Statistics
- **64% of job seekers are satisfied at work — but 82% are open to new job opportunities**
- 47% did not submit a cover letter with their most recent application
- 46% say it was harder to find a job than last year
- Job-change frequency: Single people (33%), ages 18–22 (60%), and people making less than $25K (41%) change jobs every 1–3 years

---

## 2018 Recruiter Nation Survey ("The Tipping Point")

**Sample:** ~1,000 recruiters

### Key Statistics
- **43% of recruiters have used texting to reach out to candidates; 88% report positive feedback**
- Almost **75% of recruiters noticed an increase in salary demands** over prior 12 months
- Strong conversational skills and enthusiasm decreased in importance among recruiters by 20%+ compared to prior year (as texting grows, different skills assessed)

---

## 2018 Job Seeker Nation Study

### Key Statistics
- **14% of American workers say they were bullied at work** in the past two years; 58% of those who experienced bullying didn't report it
- **66% who experienced harassment or bullying actively pursue a new job**
- 12% of candidates applied for jobs and got an interview; 28% of those interviewed received a job offer

---

## 2019 Job Seeker Nation Survey

**Sample:** ~1,500 U.S. workers (with Zogby Analytics)

### Key Statistics
- 12% of candidates applied for jobs and got an interview
- 28% of those interviewed left with a job offer

---

## 2020 Job Seeker Nation Survey

### Key Statistics
- **69% report the job seeking process is now more stressful** (pandemic impact)
- 62% of workers with children at home report stress levels increased over the last 60 days
- **64% of men believe men and women in the same roles with the same experience are paid equally** (vs. 50% of women)
- **62% of recruiters received 100 or fewer applications per average requisition** (March 2019–March 2020)
- 43% received fewer than 50 applications

---

## 2021 Recruiter Nation Report (September 2021)

**Sample:** 800+ U.S.-based recruiters and HR professionals (Zogby Analytics)

### Key Statistics
- **39% of organizations are increasing staffing levels and hiring rapidly** (13 percentage point jump from 2020)
- Nearly **80% of recruiters say their priorities have shifted** over the past year
- 40% have increased recruiting budgets; **64% expect budgets to increase** over next 6–12 months

### Diversity, Equity & Inclusion
- 40% say candidates have turned down an interview/offer due to lack of diversity
- Near half of recruiters say job seekers inquiring about D&I initiatives increased (up 16 percentage points from 2020)

### Remote/Flexible Work
- **57% believe lack of flexible/remote policies makes attracting candidates harder**
- **60% believe organizations will lose employees** if they don't transition to hybrid/remote-first culture

### Workforce Model Shifts
- 35% outsourcing more jobs to freelancers; 54% plan to outsource even more
- 40% making more internal hires to meet hiring needs

---

## 2022 Employ Recruiter Nation Report

### Key Statistics
- **51% cite more open roles as top stressor** (vs. 34% in 2023)
- 59% cite not enough qualified candidates (vs. 45% in 2023)
- **73% of recruiters report at least a moderate increase in salary negotiations** — up 20+ percentage points since 2020
- Nearly half of workers believed they could make more money by switching jobs
- **Mobile recruiting texts had 98% open rate** vs. 20% for emails
- Social media usage in recruiting: 46% in 2020 (up from 41% in 2018)
