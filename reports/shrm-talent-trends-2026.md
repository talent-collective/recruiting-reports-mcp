# SHRM — 2026 Talent Trends Report: Data-Driven Insights for the Future of Work

**Source:** https://www.shrm.org/topics-tools/research/2026-talent-trends
**Published:** 2026-05-05
**Data:** 2,094 HR professionals surveyed via SHRM Voice of Work Research Panel (Feb. 9–20, 2026)
**Period:** Survey data collected February 9–20, 2026
**Best for:** Recruiting difficulty benchmarks by HR function, skills gap quantification, talent strategy alternatives to external hiring, retention challenge scope

---

## Recruiting Challenges

- ~70% of HR professionals face challenges recruiting for full-time positions
- 53% say recruiting has become **more difficult** compared with one year ago
- 49% report a lack of qualified job candidates as a challenge; 20% identify it as their **greatest** challenge
- 77% report difficulties specifically hiring for **roles that require new skills**

## Retention and Workforce Stability

- 42% of HR professionals reported difficulty **retaining** full-time employees in the past 12 months
- Internal mobility, upskilling, and agile redeployment cited as top strategies to reduce turnover pressure on recruiting

## Skills Gap Findings

- 80% of HR professionals report the greatest difficulty finding candidates with **systems and resource management skills**: judgment, decision-making, complex problem-solving, and time management
- Report urges shift from credential-based to skills-based hiring to address persistent gaps

## Methodology

- **Source:** SHRM Voice of Work Research Panel
- **N:** 2,094 HR professionals
- **Period:** February 9–20, 2026
- **Coverage:** Organizations of all sizes and industries across the U.S.
- **Weighting:** Data not weighted
- **Published:** May 2026

## Key Conclusions

- Nearly three-quarters of HR professionals struggle to recruit for full-time roles, and the problem is worsening year-over-year.
- Skills gaps are the core driver: 77% cannot find candidates with the new technical and cognitive skills roles now require.
- Organizations using job rotation and internal mobility report 93% effectiveness in addressing talent gaps, yet fewer than 1 in 4 have implemented these programs.
- The report recommends a structural shift from volume-based external hiring to skills-based identification, internal development, and agile deployment.
- Retention is a compounding pressure: 4 in 10 HR professionals are simultaneously struggling to keep current employees while struggling to hire new ones.
