# Ashby: Recruiter Productivity | 2025 Talent Trends Report

## Key Takeaways

- Applications per hire tripled from 2021 to 2024 (~182% increase from baseline)
- After hitting a low of ~4.3 hires/recruiter in early 2023, the rate stabilized at ~5.4 in 2024; by Q1 2026, business roles hit a 5-year high of 5.0 and technical stabilized at 3.8
- Teams interviewed ~40% more candidates per hire in 2024 than in 2021
- Despite increased recruiter workload, the hired candidate's interview experience has not gotten worse — hours and events per hired candidate are stable
- Getting to the offer stage after interviewing has become more difficult; those who get there are more likely to accept

---

## Context

This report is a full revisit of the original August 2023 recruiter productivity analysis. The time window was extended from Q2 2023 to Q3 2024, allowing a 1.5-year comparison. Separate data updates through Q1 2026 are reflected in the Recruiter Productivity section.

## Part 1: The Recruiter Experience

### Hires per Recruiter

Overall trend:
- Early 2021 high: nearly 7 hires/recruiter/quarter
- Early 2023 low: ~4.3 hires/recruiter/quarter
- Climbed from 4.3 in Q1 2023 to 5.6 by Q1 2024
- Q3 2023–Q3 2024 average: **~5.4 hires per recruiter**
- By Q1 2026: **business roles = 5.0 (5-year high), technical roles = 3.8**

By function:
- Business roles consistently higher per quarter than technical roles across the full time series
- Both functions saw the same dip from Q2 2022 through Q4 2023, followed by a 2024 rebound

**Why the rebound is notable:** Layoffs reduced talent team sizes, leaving fewer recruiters managing the same volume of work. Despite higher application volume, recruiters are hiring more per quarter — suggesting teams have not just kept pace but regained momentum.

### Applications per Hire

- 2023 report: ~120% increase from 2021 baseline
- Extended to 2024: **~182% increase** from 2021 baseline (Q4 2023–Q3 2024)
- Sustained: the surge has not reversed

Context:
- Recruiting coordinator volume per org dropped starting 2022 — less scheduling support with more work
- Applications per job grew 2.6–3x at the start of 2024
- Managing inbound volume was the #1 challenge cited by talent teams throughout 2024
- Led directly to emergence of AI-assisted application review tooling

### Applications Interviewed per Hire

Overall:
- From Q2 2022 through Q1 2023: rapidly increased, then plateaued
- 2024: teams interviewed **~40% more candidates** per hire for both business and technical roles than in 2021
- Technical roles require more applications interviewed per hire than business roles

By role:

**Business roles:**
- Operations: **20.8** (highest)
- Customer Support: **8.5** (lowest)

**Technical roles:**
- Product Management: **20.6** (highest)
- Design: **15.3** (lowest)

### Interview Hours per Hire

Overall:
- Jumped ~1.3x in 2022 and remained elevated
- 2024 vs. 2021: **21% higher** for technical roles, **6% higher** for business roles
- Overall hiring processes have become more time-consuming
- Technical roles require **~14 more interview hours** than business roles

By role:
- Operations: **28.1 hours**
- Product Management: **33.6 hours** (highest)
- Customer Support: lowest

**Why the increase?** Quality of Hire has become a dominant focus — a stark shift from 2021's "hire at all costs" environment. More interviews per hire reflects deliberate evaluation, not inefficiency per se.

## Part 2: The Candidate Experience

### Application to Interview Rate

- **Technical candidates:** percentage getting an interview has **decreased** over time (though partially recovered from 2023 low)
- **Business roles:** Application to Interview Rate relatively steady after a drop from 2022 levels

### Interview to Offer Rate

- 2023 all-time lows: **~7% of technical**, **~9% of business** candidates interviewed made it to offers
- Business roles began rapid increase from Q3 2023
- By Q3 2024: somewhat stable, still below 2021 highs

**Key intersection with Offer Acceptance Rate data:** Getting an offer is increasingly difficult. But those who do get there are more likely to accept — 2023 saw the highest average OAR in the analyzed period at **81%**. In other words, companies are being more selective and candidates respond positively when they're chosen.

### Interview Hours per Hired Candidate

- **Stable** — no significant changes over the 3.5-year period analyzed
- Despite recruiters investing significantly more time and reviewing far more applications, the successful candidate's interview burden has not increased
- Average interview events per hired candidate:
  - **Technical: 4.7 events**
  - **Business: 4.0 events**
  - Both stable over 3.5 years

**Takeaway for candidates:** The amount of effort per individual role has not changed. But getting an interview requires applying to more jobs than ever, and making it to the final offer stage is harder than before.

### Hired Candidate Interview Hours Distribution

Business roles concentrated between **1–4 hours**:
- 13% of business hires: 1–1.5 hrs
- 16% of business hires: 2–2.5 hrs

Technical: more spread/varied distribution — candidate experience for technical hires is more variable than business.

Median and middle 50% range (Q2–Q3 2024):
- Technical median: **41 days** Time to Hire
- Business median: **32 days** Time to Hire

## Part 3: Traditional Recruiting Metrics

### Time to Hire

- Very stable over the past 3.5 years
- 75th percentile: **technical ≤60 days**, **business ≤50 days**
- Technical takes roughly 10 days longer than business at the 75th percentile
- Median: technical = 41 days, business = 32 days

*Note: TTH is notoriously skewed — paused/restarted processes, new job descriptions mid-search, etc. can distort individual records.*

### Time to First Fill

- Remained steady overall with turbulence in Q2 2022–Q2 2023
- Q2 2022 spike: **104 days technical**, **85 days business**
- Q2 2023 drop: **80 days technical**, **60 days business**
- Full timespan average: **technical = 88 days**, **business = 68 days**
- Technical consistently 10–20 days longer to fill than business

### Source of Hire Trends

- **Inbound applications** = most hires, peaked Q3 2023, still dominant
- **External job boards** = declined to half of 2021 levels by 2024
- **Agencies** = fewest hires alongside job boards
- Remaining ~48% of hires from: sourcing, referrals, internal transfers

**Trend:** More and more hires are coming from direct applicants. Job board hires have halved since 2021.

### Quality of Hire by Source

- Using Quality of Hire data (new hire manager surveys at 30/60/90 days) alongside Source of Hire reveals which channels produce the best in-seat performance
- Example scenario from Ashby data: inbound applied candidates had lowest QoH score (3.3/5); sourced LinkedIn candidates had highest
- This varies widely by company and role type — engineering QoH leaders may differ from marketing QoH leaders
