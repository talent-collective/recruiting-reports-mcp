# HireVue — 2026 Global AI in Hiring Report

## Methodology

- **3,100+** hiring managers surveyed worldwide
- Global scope across multiple industries and regions

---

## Key Statistics

| Metric | % |
|--------|---|
| Candidates using AI to prepare resumes | **71%** |
| HR teams employing AI regularly | **77%** |
| Hiring teams with full confidence in AI systems | **41%** |

---

## Three Central Themes for 2026

### 1. AI is now embedded infrastructure
AI has transitioned from optional tool to fundamental component of hiring. 77% of HR teams use it regularly — it is no longer an experiment.

### 2. The trust gap
Despite widespread adoption, only **41%** of hiring teams have full confidence in AI systems. The majority are using tools they don't fully trust.

### 3. The transparency imperative
Organizations must emphasize:
- Responsible AI deployment
- Explainability of AI decisions to candidates and internal teams
- Open communication about where and how AI is used in the process

---

## Key Conclusions

- Candidate-side AI use (71% using AI for resumes) is reshaping what "inbound quality" means — applications are increasingly AI-assisted
- The adoption/confidence gap (77% use vs. 41% full confidence) is the defining tension in hiring technology right now
- Transparency and explainability are becoming competitive differentiators, not just compliance requirements
- The next phase of AI in hiring is trust-building, not just tool adoption
