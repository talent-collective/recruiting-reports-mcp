# LinkedIn Global Recruiting Trends — Annual Series 2015–2020

**Source:** LinkedIn Talent Solutions (business.linkedin.com/talent-solutions)
**Data:** LinkedIn platform behavioral data (660M–700M+ members); annual global surveys of talent acquisition professionals
**Period:** 2015–2020 (annual reports)
**Best for:** Passive candidate sourcing, employer branding evolution, quality of hire measurement, soft skills trends, people analytics growth, internal mobility, multi-generational workforce

---

## 2015 Report — "Global Talent Trends 2015"

**Survey:** 20,000+ professionals across 29 countries; LinkedIn platform data from 660M+ members

### Key Statistics
- **75%+ of professionals are passive candidates** — would not proactively seek out or apply to jobs
- Only 61% of global companies have a strategy for passive candidate recruiting
- 69% of respondents cite role responsibilities as the most important piece of info in recruiter outreach
- 64% say "why recruiters are reaching out" matters in outreach
- 52% say estimated salary range is important in outreach
- 45% cite role seniority; 33% company culture; 33% company mission
- LinkedIn Career Page followers were **81% more likely to respond to InMails** than non-followers
- 58% of hiring managers believe lack of soft skills among candidates is "limiting their company's productivity"

### Top Soft Skills in Demand (2014–2015)
Communication, teamwork, critical thinking — fundamental skills applicable across every function

### Major Trends
- Passive candidate sourcing gap: most workers are passive but few companies have a strategy
- Talent brand beginning to emerge as a competitive differentiator
- Soft skills surpassing hard skills in recruiting importance

## 2016 Report — "Global Recruiting Trends 2016"

**Survey:** 3,894–4,125 talent acquisition decision makers in corporate HR across 35 countries

### Key Statistics
- **75% of talent leaders rank quality of hire** as the most important metric — but only **33% feel their measurement methodology is strong**
- Employee referrals recognized as the #1 source of quality hires (gaining on social professional networks and job boards)
- Referred employees demonstrate longer tenure and higher job performance
- Employee retention is top of mind for TA leaders
- Internal hiring is significantly lower on the priority scale — a third say it is not a high priority

### Top Sources for Quality Hires (Ranked)
1. Social professional networks
2. Job boards
3. Employee referrals
4. Third-party recruiters/staffing firms

### Major Trends
- Quality of hire replacing volume metrics as top priority
- Employee referral programs gaining strategic importance
- Disconnect between retention priority and internal hiring investment

## 2017 Report — "Global Recruiting Trends 2017"

**Survey:** 4,000–8,800 corporate talent acquisition leaders across 35–39 countries

### Key Statistics
- **56% of talent leaders say their team's hiring volume will increase**
- 79% of staffing firms predict increase in candidates placed
- **83% of talent and HR leaders say talent is a #1 priority at their company**
- 83% meet with the C-suite regularly
- 75% say their team is key to the company's workforce planning
- **80% agree employer brand has significant impact** on ability to hire great talent
- 53% would prioritize employer branding investment if budget were unconstrained

### Top Sources of Quality Hires
1. Employee referrals — 48%
2. Third-party websites/job boards — 46%
3. Social professional networks — 40%
4. Third-party recruiters — 34%
5. Internal hires — 28%

### Key Future Trends Identified
- Automation of screening and initial assessment
- Diversity as a competitive imperative
- Purpose and mission as an employer brand driver

## 2018 Report — "Global Recruiting Trends 2018"

**Survey:** 8,800+ recruiters and hiring managers globally across 39 countries

### Diversity Hiring
- **78% of talent leaders are actively pursuing diverse hiring initiatives**
- 78% prioritize diversity to improve culture
- 62% implement diversity to enhance financial performance

### New Interview Methods
- 63% assess candidate soft skills using new techniques
- 57% evaluate candidate weaknesses
- 42% address interviewer bias in new methods

### Data Utilization
- 56% use data to increase employee retention
- 50% leverage data to identify skills gaps
- 50% apply data insights to build competitive offers

### Artificial Intelligence in Recruiting
- **67% report AI saves recruiting time**
- 43% note AI removes human bias
- 31% cite improved candidate matching
- AI most valuable for: sourcing candidates (58%), screening (56%), candidate nurturing (55%)

### Additional Data
- Tech/software sector had highest talent turnover rate at **13.2%** in 2017, followed by retail, media, and professional services

## 2019 Report — "Global Talent Trends 2019"

**Survey:** LinkedIn members surveyed September–October 2018; analysis of 590M+ LinkedIn members across 200+ countries

### Soft Skills
- **92% of talent professionals and hiring managers agree candidates with strong soft skills are increasingly important**
- 89% believe "bad hires" typically lack soft skills

### Workplace Flexibility
- **72% of talent professionals consider flexibility extremely important** for the future of recruiting
- 78% increase in job posts mentioning "workplace flexibility" since 2016

### Pay Transparency
- 53% of talent professionals agree pay transparency is extremely important in shaping the future of recruiting
- 57% agree sharing salary ranges streamlines negotiations and makes hiring faster

### Anti-Harassment / Culture
- 75% noticed workplace culture changes in the past two years
- 80% report their company implemented or plans to implement anti-harassment actions within 12 months

### Four Transformative Trends Identified
1. Soft skills prioritization
2. Workplace flexibility
3. Pay transparency
4. Anti-harassment practices

## 2020 Report — "Global Talent Trends 2020"

**Survey:** 7,000+ talent professionals in 35 countries; LinkedIn behavioral data from 660M+ members

### Employee Experience
- **96% of HR/TA leaders say creating a great employee experience is becoming more important** for retention and the bottom line
- Only **52% of HR/TA leaders say their company provides a positive employee experience**
- Top five areas for improvement: compensation/benefits, simplified administrative processes, open/effective management, intuitive tools/technologies, ongoing employee training

### People Analytics
- **73% of talent leaders say embracing people analytics will be a major priority** in the next 5 years
- 55% of companies say they still need help with basic people analytics
- **242% increase in HR professionals with data analysis skills** over the past 5 years

### Internal Recruiting
- Companies with higher-than-average internal hiring see **employees stay 41% longer**
- Internal recruiting has increased by **10% since 2015**

### Multigenerational Workforce
- 89% of talent professionals say a multigenerational workforce makes a company more successful
