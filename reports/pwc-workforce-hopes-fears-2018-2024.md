# PwC Workforce of the Future & Global Workforce Hopes and Fears — 2018–2024

## 2018 — "Workforce of the Future: The Competing Forces Shaping 2030"

**Research:** Begun in 2007; specially commissioned survey
**Sample:** 10,000 people in China, India, Germany, UK, and US

### Key Statistics
- **73% of respondents are optimistic about the future of work**
- **65% believe technology will enhance job prospects**
- 88% of Indians think technology will improve job prospects (vs. only **40% in UK**)
- Only **1 in 4 CEOs describe it as "somewhat" or "very easy" to attract digital talent**
- 60% of Chinese CEOs: attracting digital talent is "somewhat" or "very easy"
- 30% of U.S. CEOs find it easy to attract digital talent
- Only 13% in Germany
- **70% want to work for an organization with a powerful social conscience**
- **75% of respondents willing to take initiative in updating their own skills**

## 2022 — Global Workforce Hopes and Fears Survey

**Published:** 2022
**Methodology:** Online survey conducted March 2022
**Sample:** 52,195 workers in 44 countries and territories

### Compensation and Mobility
- **35% planning to ask their employer for more money** in the next 12 months
- 44% in tech sector plan to ask for a raise; 25% in public sector
- **1 in 5 workers (20%) likely to switch employers** in the next 12 months

### Job Change Motivators
1. Pay increase — 71%
2. Fulfilling job — 69%
3. Being themselves at work — 66%

### Pay Equity
- Women were **7 points less likely than men to say they are fairly rewarded financially**, but also **7 points less likely to ask for a raise**

### Gen Z
- Gen Z workers less satisfied with jobs; **2x more likely than Baby Boomers to worry technology will replace their role**

### Purpose
- **75% of employees want to work for organization making positive contribution to society**

### Discrimination
- **50% report experiencing discrimination at work**

## 2023 — Global Workforce Hopes and Fears Survey

**Published:** 2023
**Sample:** Nearly 54,000 workers in 46 countries and territories

### Key Statistics
- **26% of employees likely to change jobs in the next 12 months** (up from 19% previous year — a 7-point jump)

### Most Likely to Change Employers
- Feeling overworked: 44%
- Struggling to pay bills monthly: 38%
- Gen Z: 35%

### Job Satisfaction Gap
- Among those likely to change employers: only **47% find their jobs fulfilling** (vs. 57% of those unlikely to change)

### Organizational Transformation
- **33% believe their company won't survive more than 10 years** without transformation

## 2024 — Global Workforce Hopes and Fears Survey

**Published:** 2024
**Sample:** 56,000+ workers in 50 countries and territories

### Work Intensity
- **45% of workers report rising workloads**
- **62% say they experienced more change at work** in the past year
- **40% report daily responsibilities changed to a large or very large extent**

### AI Impact on Workers
- **76% of all AI users expected AI to create opportunities to learn new skills** at work
- **82% of daily GenAI users** expected it to make their work more efficient in next 12 months

### Daily GenAI Users vs. Infrequent Users
| Benefit | Daily Users | Infrequent Users |
|---------|------------|-----------------|
| Productivity benefits | 92% | 58% |
| Job security benefits | 58% | 36% |
| Salary benefits | 52% | 32% |
