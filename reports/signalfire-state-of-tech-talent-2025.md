# SignalFire — State of Tech Talent Report 2025

## Methodology
Proprietary Beacon AI platform data tracking 650+ million professionals and 80+ million organizations. Big Tech defined as top 15 companies by market cap. Startups defined as top 100 VC firms, Seed-Series C rounds in previous 4 years. Top CS grads = top 20 engineering programs per US News rankings.

## New Graduate Hiring Crisis

- Big Tech: new grads now represent just 7% of hires (down 25% from 2023, down 50% from 2019)
- Startups: new grads under 6% of hires (down 11% from 2023, down 30% from 2019)
- New college grad unemployment increased 30% since September 2022
- 55% of employers believe Gen Z struggles with teamwork
- 37% of managers prefer using AI over hiring Gen Z employees
- Top CS graduates seeing dramatic decline landing roles at Magnificent Seven (Alphabet, Amazon, Apple, Meta, Microsoft, NVIDIA, Tesla)

**Root cause:** Not primarily AI — it's the end of pandemic-era low interest rate funding (2020-2022) that fueled over-hiring. Series A tech startups are now 20% smaller than 2020, with shorter runways forcing leaner hiring and less investment in training.

## AI Lab Talent Retention (2-year benchmark)

| Company | 2-Year Retention |
|---------|-----------------|
| Anthropic | 80% (highest in industry) |
| DeepMind | 78% |
| OpenAI | 67% |
| Meta | 64% |

- Engineers 8x more likely to leave OpenAI for Anthropic than vice versa
- Anthropic has nearly 11:1 advantage over DeepMind departures
- Primary sources of Anthropic talent: Google, Meta, Microsoft, Amazon, Stripe
- Anthropic, OpenAI, and Meta growing engineering teams 2-3x faster than losing them

## Geographic Talent Shifts

**Growing:**
- San Francisco and NYC: over 65% of AI engineers concentrated here
- Miami: 12% jump in AI roles (tax perks and lifestyle)
- San Diego: 7% rise in Big Tech roles; $5.7B startup funding in 2024

**Cooling:**
- Austin: 6% drop in VC-backed startup headcount
- Houston: 10.9% decline in startup talent

## Hiring Composition Changes

- Big Tech doubling down on ML and data engineering
- Non-technical functions shrinking: recruiting, product, sales
- Companies posting junior roles but filling with senior individual contributors due to lean budgets and reduced training investment

## 2025 Predictions

- Rise of generalist engineers (tools like Copilot, Replit, Cursor reducing need for deep ML expertise)
- Boom in fractional executives and equity advisors
- New roles emerging: AI governance leads, AI ethics specialists, agentic AI engineers, non-human security ops
