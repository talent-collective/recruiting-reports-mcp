# ManpowerGroup Talent Shortage Survey — Annual Series 2015–2024

**Source:** ManpowerGroup (manpowergroup.com)
**Data:** Annual global survey of employers; approximately 39,000–42,000 employers in 41–43 countries
**Period:** 2015–2024 (annual reports since 2006)
**Best for:** Global and country-level talent shortage trends, hardest-to-fill roles, employer response strategies, skills gap data, historical shortage trajectory

## 2015 — 10th Annual Report

**Sample:** ~42,000 employers across 42+ countries

### U.S. Statistics
- **32% of U.S. employers report difficulties filling vacancies** (down from 40% in 2014)
- 48% acknowledge talent shortages have medium to high business impact
- **20% are not pursuing strategies to address shortages** (up from 13% in 2014)
- 43% report negative impact on meeting client needs

### Hardest Roles to Fill (U.S.) — 6th Consecutive Year at #1
1. Skilled trade workers
2. Drivers
3. Teachers
4. Sales representatives
5. Administrative professionals

### Reasons for Difficulty
- Lack of applicants: 33%
- Lack of experience: 19%
- Lack of technical competencies: 17%

### Global Statistics
- **38% of global employers experience shortages** (up from 36% in 2014)
- Japan leads globally with **83% experiencing shortages**
- Peru (68%) and Hong Kong (65%) also high
- Brazil and Romania at 61%

---

## 2016 — 11th Annual Report

**Sample:** 42,000+ employers in 43 countries and territories

### Key Statistics
- **40% of employers globally have difficulty filling positions**
- Talent shortages worsened in **6 of the 9 largest global economies** year-over-year
- More than half of employers globally are developing and training their own people to overcome skill gaps

---

## 2018 — 13th Annual Report (Record High)

**Sample:** 39,195 employers across 43 countries and territories

### Key Statistics
- **45% of employers worldwide struggle to fill roles** — highest shortage rate in 12 years at the time
- 27% of employers report applicants lack required hard or soft skills
- U.S. grew from 32% (2015) to **46% in 2018**

### Most Affected Countries
| Country | Shortage Rate |
|---------|--------------|
| Japan | 89% |
| Romania | 81% |
| Taiwan | 78% |
| Argentina | 52% |

### Least Affected
| Country | Shortage Rate |
|---------|--------------|
| Ireland | 18% |
| UK | 19% |
| Netherlands | 24% |

### Employer Response
- **56% of employers investing in workforce training** (double the rate from four years prior)
- Strategy framework: "Build, Buy, Borrow, Bridge"

---

## 2019 — Published January 2020

**Sample:** 41,700+ employers across 42 countries

### Key Statistics
- **54% of companies report skills shortages** — nearly double the rate from a decade earlier
- Businesses in **36 of 44 countries** finding it more difficult than in 2018
- **U.S.: 69% of employers report shortages** (highest among major economies)
- Mexico: 52%; Italy: 47%; Spain: 41%

### Worker Priorities by Generation
| Generation | Top Priority |
|------------|-------------|
| Gen Z women | Compensation (2x rate of skills development) |
| Gen Z men | Skills and career advancement (nearly equal to pay) |
| Millennials | Flexibility and challenging roles |
| Gen X | Work-life balance, remote work capabilities |
| Boomers | Compensation, challenging assignments, flexibility |
| Workers 65+ | Purpose-driven work |

### Top Worker Priorities Across Demographics
1. Autonomy regarding work location and timing
2. Wellbeing supporting work-life balance
3. Career mobility and skill-building
4. Strong leadership
5. Organizational purpose

---

## 2022 Report

**Key Statistics**
- **75% of employers globally reported talent shortages** (up from 69% in 2021)
- Top 5 most in-demand skills: IT & data, engineering, sales & marketing, operations & logistics, manufacturing & production

---

## 2023 Report

**Sample:** Nearly 39,000 employers across 41 countries

### Key Statistics
- **77% of employers report difficulty filling roles** — highest level since survey began in 2006
- **57% of organizations plan to offer increased flexibility** for when or where work occurs
- Strong hiring demand combined with rapidly evolving skill requirements creating unprecedented talent scarcity

### Most Affected Countries
| Country | Shortage Rate |
|---------|--------------|
| Japan | 85% |
| Germany, Greece, Israel | 82% |
| Ireland, Portugal | 81% |

### Employer Strategy
- "Build, Buy, Borrow, Bridge" talent framework continues as recommended approach

---

## 2024 Report

### Key Statistics
- **75% of employers globally report talent shortages** (slight decrease from 77% in 2023)
- IT & data skills remain most challenging to find across all industries and geographies
- Health Care and Life Sciences report the most difficulty

### Employer Responses
- Offering more work flexibility: 65%
- Raising wages: 30%
- Looking at new talent pools: 28%
