# Korn Ferry Talent Acquisition & Workforce Trends — 2017–2024

**Source:** Korn Ferry (kornferry.com/insights)
**Data:** Global surveys of HR professionals, talent leaders, and labor market analysis
**Period:** 2017–2024
**Best for:** Global talent crunch projections, skills shortage economic impact, purpose-driven work, AI in talent acquisition, geographic mobility, interim talent, skills-based hiring momentum

---

## 2017 — Talent Trend Predictions

**Published:** Early 2017

### Key Statistics
- **73% of respondents said their #1 driver at work is meaning and purpose**
- Only **3% said pay was the top driver**
- Economic analysis: human capital represents potential value of **$1.2 quadrillion** to the global economy (2.33x that of physical capital)
- By 2020, as much as **40% of the American workforce projected to be contingent workers** (estimate cited in report)

## 2018 — "Future of Work: The Global Talent Crunch"

**Published:** May 2018
**Partners:** Man Bites Dog and Oxford Analytica
**Methodology:** Analysis of labor supply-demand gaps in 20 economies across three sectors (financial/business services; tech/media/telecom; manufacturing)

### Headline Projections (by 2030)
- **85.2 million unfilled jobs globally by 2030** due to talent shortage
- **$8.5 trillion in unrealized annual revenues** by 2030 if unchecked
- U.S. could lose **$1.748 trillion annually** by 2030 (approximately 6% of U.S. economy)

### Sector-Specific Shortages by 2030
| Sector | Worker Deficit | Annual Revenue Loss |
|--------|--------------|---------------------|
| Financial & Business Services | 10.7 million | $1.3T |
| Technology, Media & Telecom | 4.3 million | — |
| Manufacturing | 7.9 million | — |

### Countries at Highest Risk by 2020
Australia, France, Germany, Japan, United States
- Combined opportunity cost: **$1.876 trillion annually**

### Exception
- India remains the only major economy maintaining a **talent surplus** through 2030

### Root Cause
- Demographics: low birth rates in Japan and European nations; U.S. baby boomers retiring without sufficient replacement pipeline
- U.S. tech sector specifically could lose **$162 billion annually** without more high-tech workers

## 2020 — Talent Trends

**Published:** 2020

### Key Statistics
- **59% of professionals said the principal driver at work is belief that their work has purpose and meaning**
- 96% agreed companies see long-term financial benefit from commitment to purpose-driven leadership
- Despite historically low unemployment (pre-COVID), companies took a more cautious approach to hiring — prioritizing roles with direct business impact (R&D, sales) over traditional service roles

---

## 2023 — Talent Acquisition Trends Report

**Published:** 2023

### Key Statistics
- One-third of **3,000 surveyed HR professionals said empathy was lacking at the top** (affecting hiring and retention)
- **Less than 2% of Americans moved for work in Q1 2023** (vs. 45% in 1986) — geographic immobility now a defining talent market challenge
- AI will handle time-consuming tasks while recruiters focus on candidate experience

---

## 2024 — Talent Acquisition Trends Report

**Published:** October 2023 (for 2024 outlook)
**Methodology:** Survey of recruiting professionals and analysis of labor market data

### AI in Talent Acquisition
- **82% of staffing leaders expect AI to have a significant impact on their business**
- **73% of leaders will be closely monitoring AI tools for potential negative effects**

### Skills-Based Hiring
- **Recruiters on LinkedIn search for candidates by skills 5x more frequently than by degrees**
- LinkedIn job postings not requiring a degree **increased 36%** in the past year
- Early-career hiring from non-traditional sources (vocational schools, high schools) growing

### Interim/Contingent Talent
- **61% of interim workers say bringing niche skills to organizations through periods of change is valuable**
- Generative AI specialists in high demand with premium pay
