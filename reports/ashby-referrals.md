# Ashby — Are Referred Candidates More Likely to Be Hired?

## Application Source Breakdown

| Source | Share of Applications |
|--------|----------------------|
| Inbound | **93.8%** |
| Sourced | 4.8% |
| Referral | 1.0% |
| Agency | 0.4% |
| Internal | 0.05% |

**Referral volume trend:** Declined from 2% (Q1 2021) to under 1% (Q1 2024)

---

## Interview & Offer Conversion Rates

| Source | Application → Interview | Interview → Offer |
|--------|------------------------|-------------------|
| Referred | **40%** | 16% |
| Internal | **42%** | 32% |
| Agency | 42% | 8% |

- Referred and internal candidates advance to interview at ~3–4x the rate of inbound applicants
- Internal candidates have the strongest interview-to-offer conversion (32%)

---

## Inbound Offer Rate Decline

- Inbound offer rate dropped ~**70%** from 2021 to 2024 (from ~7 to ~2 per 1,000 applications)
- Despite this, inbound remains the dominant hiring source due to sheer volume

---

## Key Conclusions

- Referred candidates advance to interviews at significantly higher rates — validated by employee endorsement
- But referrals represent only ~1% of applications, limiting their absolute hiring impact
- Internal candidates show the strongest overall conversion (42% → interview, 32% → offer)
- Agency candidates advance to interview well (42%) but convert to offers poorly (8%)
- Inbound's offer rate has compressed sharply as application volume surged
