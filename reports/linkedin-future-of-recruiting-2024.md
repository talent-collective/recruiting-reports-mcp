# LinkedIn Future of Recruiting 2024

**Source:** LinkedIn Talent Solutions (business.linkedin.com/talent-solutions)
**Published:** 2024
**Methodology:** 1,453 recruiting professionals surveyed across management levels; 498 hiring managers; 23 countries; six languages (October–November 2023); 16,688 global members surveyed for candidate priorities (November 2023)
**Best for:** AI adoption benchmarks, skills-based hiring data, flexible work impact on recruiting, Gen Z dynamics, quality of hire measurement

---

## AI Adoption in Recruiting

- **27% of talent professionals currently use or experiment with generative AI**
- **60% of recruiting pros express optimism about AI in recruitment**
- **14% jump in recruiters adding AI skills to their LinkedIn profiles** year-over-year
- Primary benefit: accelerating job description writing and automating routine tasks
- Among early GAI users: 35% use time saved for candidate screening; 26% use it for skill assessments

---

## Skills-Based Hiring

- **10x increase in talent pool potential** when focusing on skills over credentials
- 80% of surveyed recruiting professionals committed to building diverse workforces
- **36% surge in LinkedIn job postings omitting degree requirements** (2019–2022)
- **24% higher InMail acceptance rates** for recruiters using skills-first search approaches

---

## Flexible Work Impact

- **16% greater likelihood of candidate InMail acceptance** at companies with flex policies
- **29% higher application rates** for employers offering flexible arrangements
- 10% increased importance of flexible work as EVP (2021–2023)
- 14% heightened importance among Gen Z specifically

---

## Gen Z Priorities

- **36% more likely to prioritize advancement opportunities** versus other generations
- **34% more inclined to seek skill development**

---

## Quality of Hire

- **89% of TA pros agree measuring quality of hire will become increasingly important**
- Only **25% feel highly confident in their organization's ability to measure quality of hire**
- **61% believe AI can improve how they measure quality of hire**

## LinkedIn Global Talent Trends 2022 (Platform Data)

**Data period:** 2021–2022

### Internal Mobility
- 16 of 19 global industries saw an increase in internal mobility (promotions and internal role transfers) in the past year
- **Employees who have moved internally have a 64% chance of remaining after 3 years** vs. 45% for those without an internal move
- Employees at companies with high internal mobility **stay almost 2x longer**
- Those who found new roles internally are **3.5x more likely to be engaged**

### Hiring Rate Declines (September 2021 vs. September 2022)
| Country/Region | Decline |
|----------------|---------|
| U.S. and Brazil | -28% |
| India | -18% |
| Switzerland | -19% |
| Singapore | -14% to -42% |

Every global region saw hiring rates drop.

### Skills Data
- Skill sets for jobs changed by **~25% since 2015**
- Expected to reach **40% change by 2025**

### Retention
- Attrition costs organizations **one-half to two times an employee's annual salary**
- Companies investing in employee skill-building see **nearly 7% higher retention rate** at 3-year mark

## LinkedIn Global Talent Trends May 2023 (Platform Analysis)

- Internal mobility increased **6% year-over-year**
- Only 1 in 10 global executives report their organization has broad leadership alignment, comprehensive tools, and strong processes for generative AI adoption
- 2.5 out of 10 executives said their companies haven't even started on generative AI adoption
- **AI-related hiring surged 323% over the previous eight years**
- Employees skilled at using generative AI are **5x more likely to develop skills** like creative ideation, design thinking, and emotional intelligence
- Companies with more employees skilled at using GAI see **4x higher leadership promotion rate** and **5x higher overall promotion rate**
- Global demand for green talent outstripped supply (2023–2024): demand increased 11.6% while supply increased only 5.6%
- Jobseekers with green skills receive a **54.6% higher hiring rate** than the rest of the global workforce
- **Skills-first approach expands eligible talent pools by nearly 10x**
