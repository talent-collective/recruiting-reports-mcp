# Mercer Global Talent Trends — Annual Series 2019–2024

**Source:** Mercer (Marsh McLennan) (mercer.com/insights)
**Data:** Annual global surveys of C-suite executives, HR leaders, and employees
**Period:** 2019–2024
**Best for:** Burnout rates, worker-employer trust, automation/reskilling readiness, financial wellbeing, compensation strategy, DEI effectiveness, hybrid work impact, high-growth company practices

---

## 2019 — "Global Talent Trends 2019"

**Published:** February 2019
**Sample:** 7,300+ senior business executives, HR leaders, and employees; 9 key industries; 16 geographies
(800+ executives, 1,600 HR leaders, 4,800 employees)

### Key Statistics
- **99% of companies are taking action to prepare for the future of work**
- **73% of executives predict significant disruption in the next three years** (vs. 26% in 2018 — nearly 3x increase)
- **60% of companies plan to automate more work** in the next 12 months
- 65% of employees are asking for more clearly defined responsibilities

### Four Top Trends for 2019
1. Aligning Work to Future Value
2. Building Brand Resonance
3. Curating the Work Experience
4. Delivering Talent-led Change

## 2020 — "Global Talent Trends 2020"

**Published:** March 2020
**Sample:** 7,300+ senior business executives, HR leaders, and employees; 9 industries; 16 geographies

### Job Security and Automation
- **34% of employees expect their jobs to be replaced in 3 years**
- 61% believe their employers are preparing them for the future of work
- **55% trust their organization to reskill them** if their job changes due to automation

### Financial Wellbeing
- **78% of employees want long-term financial planning support**
- Only **23% of companies provide financial education** for employees

### Transparency Impact
- Employees at companies transparent about which jobs will change are more likely to be thriving (**72% vs. 56%**)

### Career Development
- **73% of employees value personal and professional growth opportunities most** among career support options

### Corporate Purpose
- **85% of executives agree organizational purpose should extend beyond shareholder primacy**
- Only **35% of companies deliver on this today**

## 2022 — "The Rise of the Relatable Organization"

**Published:** April 2022
**Sample:** Nearly 11,000 C-suite executives, HR leaders, and employees; 16 countries; 13 industries

### Burnout
- **81% of employees feel at risk of burnout** in 2022 (up from 63% in 2020)
- Top reason for burnout: not feeling sufficiently rewarded for their efforts

### Remote/Hybrid Work
- More than half of employees want to work remotely or hybrid
- **72% of executives worry about impact of remote work on company culture**

### Turnover
- **70% of HR professionals expect higher rates of turnover** in 2022

### Core Theme
- Four in five C-suite executives believe the people and business agendas have never been more intertwined
- "The Rise of the Relatable Organization" — organizations must connect individual purpose to corporate strategy

## 2023 — "Global Talent Trends 2023"

**Published:** 2023 (88-page report)
**Sample:** 11,000+ individuals; 50+ CEOs/CFOs; 3,000+ HR leaders; 4,000+ employees

### Key Statistics
- **81% of workers feel at risk of burnout** (same rate as 2022)
- **60% of workers say their company doesn't meet their needs**
- **77% of surveyed organizations modifying rewards strategy** to address inflation
- Key concern: "employee exhaustion" identified as top risk

### Worker Complaints (Factors Contributing to Exhaustion)
- Toxic work cultures
- Unbearable workloads
- Unfairness
- Lack of support network

### DEI
- DEI programs proving "stubbornly difficult to unlock"

### Approaches to Skills Shortages
1. Deconstructing jobs into tasks
2. Automating parts of jobs
3. Redesigning work models
4. Accessing non-traditional talent pools
5. Upskilling/reskilling existing workforce

## 2024 — "Global Talent Trends 2024"

**Published:** 2024 (68-page report)
**Sample:** 12,200+ respondents; 17 geographies; 16 industries (C-suite executives, HR leaders, employees, investors)

### Trust
- **Trust in employers fell from an all-time high in 2022**
- **82% of employees are concerned they will burn out**

### High-Growth Companies vs. Lower-Growth Peers
- **51% of high-growth companies redesigned work for employee well-being** (vs. 39% of lower-growth peers)

### AI and Workforce Transformation
- **74% of executives concerned about talent's ability to pivot**
- **Less than 28% of HR leaders very confident** in making human-machine teaming a success
- **58% of executives worry company isn't doing enough to inspire workers** to adopt new technologies
- **67% of HR leaders concerned they implemented new technology without transforming work**

### Retention
- Top reasons workers intend to stay: fair pay (34%), development opportunities (28%)

### Productivity Barriers
- "Too much busy work lacking value"
- "Too many interruptions / not enough thinking time"
