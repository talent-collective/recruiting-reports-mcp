# NACE — Job Outlook 2026 Spring Update

**Source:** https://www.naceweb.org/research/reports/2026/job-outlook/spring-update/
**Published:** 2026-04-27
**Data:** 185 employer respondents (142 NACE employer members representing 19.9% of eligible members; 43 non-member companies)
**Period:** Survey conducted February 12 – March 17, 2026; covers college Class of 2026 hiring plans
**Best for:** Entry-level/college graduate hiring projections, skills-based hiring adoption, employer sentiment on graduate preparedness, internship and co-op value benchmarks

---

## Hiring Projections for Class of 2026

- Employers project a **5.6% increase** in new college graduate hiring for the Class of 2026 vs. Class of 2025
  - This is a significant upward revision from the fall 2025 projection of only **1.6%** growth
- Largest companies (5,000+ employees) are driving the gains: **+8.7%** hiring increase
- Hiring gains are primarily concentrated at larger employers; small/mid-size employer projections are more modest

## Market Assessment

- **45%** of employers characterize the overall job market for Class of 2026 graduates as "fair" (plurality)
- **20%+** of employers rate new college graduates as "very well prepared" for the roles they will enter

## Skills-Based Hiring Adoption

- **70%** of employers report using skills-based hiring — up from **65%** the prior year
- **71%** use skills-based hiring at least half the time across their requisitions
- Employers value hands-on experience even as skills-based approaches gain traction

## Candidate Preparation and Experience Preferences

| Experience Type | Employer Value Rating |
|---|---|
| U.S.-based internships | Nearly 100% (near-universal value) |
| Co-ops | ~76% find value |
| On-campus student work | ~40% actively seek |
| Apprenticeships | ~40% actively seek |

## AI and Early Career Work

- AI is increasingly becoming an expectation for early career talent
- Employers are reshaping entry-level role definitions to incorporate AI tool use and human-AI collaboration
- The shift is influencing both the skills employers seek and the nature of early career work itself

## Key Conclusions

- College graduate hiring is recovering: the 5.6% spring projection nearly quadruples the 1.6% fall estimate, suggesting employer confidence improved meaningfully from fall 2025 to early 2026
- Large employers are disproportionately driving the recovery — smaller organizations remain cautious
- Skills-based hiring has crossed majority adoption (70%) and is now the modal approach among NACE employer members
- Internship experience remains nearly universally valued — organizations that maintain and grow internship programs retain a strong talent pipeline advantage
- AI fluency is becoming an implicit expectation for entry-level candidates, with employers reshaping role requirements around human-AI collaboration
