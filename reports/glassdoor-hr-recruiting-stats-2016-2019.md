# Glassdoor HR & Recruiting Statistics Reports — 2016–2019

## 2016 — "50 HR & Recruiting Stats"

**Data:** Glassdoor proprietary research and third-party HR studies

### Application Volume
- On average, **each corporate job opening attracts 250 résumés**
- **4–6 candidates called for interview**; **1 offered the job**

### Social Media & Mobile
- **79% of candidates use social media in their job search**; 86% in the first 10 years of their career
- Nearly 2 in 3 employees say their employer does not (or doesn't know how to) use social media to promote job openings
- **45% of job seekers use their mobile devices to search for jobs at least once a day**

### Diversity
- **67% of job seekers say it's important that a company has a diverse workforce**
- 57% of employees think their company should be doing more to increase diversity

### Employer Brand & Reviews
- Majority of candidates **read 6 reviews** before forming an opinion about a company
- **70% of people look to reviews before making career decisions**
- **62% say their perception of a company improves** after seeing an employer respond to a review
- 67% of employers believed conveying a clearer picture about working at a company before hiring would improve retention rates

### Employee Engagement ROI
- Increasing employee engagement investments by **10% can increase profits by $2,400 per employee per year**

## 2017 — "50 HR & Recruiting Stats"

### Key Statistics
- Making the interview process **10% harder results in 2.6% higher satisfaction** by the new employee
- **75% of active job seekers are likely to apply to a job** if the employer actively manages its employer brand
- **Companies investing in employer branding are 3x more likely to make quality hires**
- Positive reviews and active employer engagement **can increase application rates by up to 70%**
- **69% of candidates would reject a job offer from a company with a negative employer brand** even if unemployed

## 2018 — "65 HR & Recruiting Stats"

**Format:** Statistical Reference Guide for Recruiters (eBook)

### Key Statistics
- Only **4% of HR managers think they know how to manage Millennials**
- **90% of Millennials prefer benefits over a salary increase**
- 67% of employers believed conveying a clearer working picture before hiring would improve retention
- All it takes is **reading 7 reviews** before applicants form an opinion about the company
- **Companies that invest in a strong hiring experience realize 70% improvement in quality of hires**
- Interview process: making it **10% harder results in 2.6% higher satisfaction** from new employee (consistent with 2017)

## 2019 — "50 HR & Recruiting Stats"

### Key Statistics
- **83% of job seekers research company reviews and ratings before deciding where to apply on Glassdoor**
- **73% of job seekers value company culture over salary**
- Candidates typically **read at least 6 reviews** before forming an opinion about a company

## 2019 Annual Job Market Trends Report

**Published:** December 2018 (for 2019 trends)

### 2019 U.S. Labor Market
- Gig economy comprises only **approximately 1% of total U.S. employment** (significantly below earlier projections)
- In 2018, **nearly half (43%) of all open jobs at tech employers on Glassdoor were for non-technical roles**
- Over **70% of job applications are for positions in applicants' home metro areas** (demonstrating importance of local talent pools)
- U.S. Census projects more retirees than children under 18 by 2035
- Job gains decreased from average of 223,000/month in 2018 to 167,000/month in early 2019

### Five Hiring Disruptions Predicted for 2019
1. Personalized job search through machine learning
2. Surge in non-tech hiring by tech companies
3. Workforce aging and talent shortages by 2035
4. Potential recession indicators emerging
5. Diversity and inclusion becoming cultural imperative

## Glassdoor Job Market Monthly Data (2018–2020)

**Launched:** April 2019 (monthly reports)
**Archive:** December 2017 through November 2020

### April 2019 Snapshot
- Nearly **5.6 million open online jobs in the U.S.** (down 0.4% from April 2018)
- Pay up **1.4% year-over-year**; average U.S. full-time worker earning median base pay of **$52,807**
- Internet & tech industry job openings **down 28.5% year-over-year** to 69,129 openings
- Consumer electronics and insurance showed fastest annual growth in open jobs
