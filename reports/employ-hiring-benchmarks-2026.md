# Employ Inc.: 2026 Hiring Benchmarks Report — "Redefining What Good Looks Like"

## Key Findings

### Speed
- Companies are screening candidates **13% faster** year-over-year
- Roles filled **4 days quicker** than last year
- Median time-to-fill dropped from **67.7 days (2025) → 63.5 days (2026)**

### Application Volume
- Applications up **24%** year-over-year
- Despite higher volume, fewer candidates are advancing past initial screens — highlighting importance of precise matching

### Candidate Experience
- Candidate experience scores average **2.9 out of 5**
- Business services and hospitality rank highest
- **Software & technology ranks last**

### Early Retention ("The Great Stay")
- First-year turnover dropped from **23.7% (2024) → 12.1% (2025)** — a **48.9% decrease**
- Consistent with BLS data showing flat unemployment and flat quits (people staying in roles)
- Signals organizations are getting onboarding right

### Recruitment Marketing Engagement
- Email engagement rates declined from **1.2% (2024) → 0.8% (2025)**
- Fewer people clicking through on recruitment marketing emails
- Companies need to get more creative in outreach

## Notable Context
- Staffing agencies: screening speed improvements raise the bar for agencies to maintain their speed advantage
- The 49% drop in first-year turnover aligns with the "Great Stay" phenomenon — employees staying put rather than moving
- Per CPO Stephanie Manzelli: "Hiring rarely feels easy regardless of market conditions, which makes it hard to define what 'good' recruiting actually looks like"
