# McKinsey — The Future of Work After COVID-19 & The Great Attrition 2021–2022

## "The Future of Work After COVID-19" (February 2021)

**Published:** February 2021
**Methodology:** Analysis across 8 focus countries: China, France, Germany, India, Japan, Spain, UK, US

### Job Transitions
- More than **100 million workers, or 1 in 16**, will need to find a different occupation by 2030 in the post-COVID-19 scenario
- 12% more than estimated before the pandemic; **up to 25% more in advanced economies**

### Remote Work
- **20–25% of workforces in advanced economies** could work from home 3–5 days per week
- This represents **4–5x more remote work** than before the pandemic
- **More than 25% of workers say they would consider switching employers** if required to return fully on-site
- **9 out of 10 organizations will combine remote and on-site working**

### Automation Acceleration
- Work arenas with high levels of human interaction likely to see the **greatest acceleration of automation and AI adoption**

### Job Market Changes
- Largest negative impact sectors: food service, customer sales and service, less-skilled office support
- Job growth areas: warehouse workers (e-commerce), wind turbine technicians (green economy), nurses/home health aides (aging populations), teachers/training instructors

### Skills
- **Roughly half of workers will need new, more advanced skills** to move to occupations one or two wage brackets higher

### Business Travel
- An estimated **20% of business travel may never recover** as virtual meetings replace in-person meetings

## "The Great Attrition" Research Series (2021–2022)

**Published:** 2021–2022 (series of articles and surveys)

### The Scale of Voluntary Exits
- **40% of employees are at least somewhat likely to quit in the next 3–6 months** (2021 survey)
- Between April and September 2021: more than **19 million U.S. workers resigned**
- November 2021: record **4.5 million workers quit** their jobs
- October 2022: around **4 million Americans quit** (2.6% of workforce)
- **Voluntary quit rate: 25% higher than pre-pandemic levels**

### Worker Motivations for Quitting
- **54% of employees who quit felt their employers didn't value them**
- 40% share of workers planning to leave their jobs remained unchanged from 2021 through 2022
- 18% said intentions ranged from likely to almost certain

### Industry Mobility
- **Majority of employees who quit between April 2020–April 2022 did not return to the same industry**
- **48% of job leavers were quitting and going to different employers in different industries**

### Top Reasons for Quitting
1. Lack of career development/advancement
2. Inadequate compensation
3. Uncaring or uninspiring leaders

## 2023–2024 McKinsey Workforce Data

### U.S. Labor Market (2021)
- As of mid-2021: **11.3 million open jobs** in the United States (up from 9.3 million in April 2021)
- Voluntary quit rate remained **25% higher than pre-pandemic levels** into 2023

### Key Recommendations
- Employers should expand talent pools with candidates skilled through alternative routes (STARs — Skilled Through Alternative Routes)
- Gen AI reshaping recruiting: helping managers write better job requirements and match candidates with skills pools
