# CareerBuilder Annual Hiring Surveys — 2016–2019

**Source:** CareerBuilder (in partnership with Harris Poll)
**Data:** Online surveys of U.S. hiring managers, HR professionals, and workers
**Period:** 2016–2019
**Best for:** Bad hire costs, hiring forecasts, skills gap data, turnover costs, why bad hires happen, employer hiring intentions, candidate fit assessment

---

## 2016 Annual Hiring Forecast

### Key Statistics
- **36% of employers planned to add full-time, permanent employees** in 2016 (same as 2015)
- Nearly half of employers (47%) planned to hire temporary/contract workers
- Mid-year update: **50% planned full-time hiring** (vs. 49% prior year)
- **76% of full-time employed workers open to new opportunities**
- **48% of employers couldn't find workers needed** to fill vacancies
- **53% said a resume didn't provide enough information** to assess a good fit
- Skills gap persisted as key challenge

## 2017 — "Bad Hire" Study

**Published:** September 2017
**Methodology:** Online survey August 16–September 15, 2017
**Sample:** 2,257 full-time hiring managers/HR professionals + 3,697 full-time workers; U.S. private sector

### The Scale of Bad Hires
- **74% of employers have hired the wrong person** for a position
- **Average cost of a bad hire: $14,900**
- **Average cost of losing a good employee: $29,600**

### Impact of Bad Hires
| Impact | % Reporting |
|--------|-------------|
| Reduced productivity | 37% |
| Time spent recruiting/training replacement | 32% |
| Compromised work quality | 31% |

### Warning Signs of a Bad Hire
| Sign | % Reporting |
|------|-------------|
| Poor quality work | 54% |
| Negative attitude | 53% |
| Doesn't work well with team | 50% |
| Attendance problems | 46% |
| Skills didn't match claims | 45% |

### Why Bad Hires Happen
| Reason | % of Employers |
|--------|----------------|
| Candidate lacked skills but seemed trainable | 35% |
| Candidate misrepresented qualifications | 33% |
| Hired based on personality alone | 32% |
| Pressure to fill role quickly | 30% |
| Difficulty finding qualified candidates | 29% |

### Worker Perspective
- **66% accepted a job later realizing it was wrong fit**
- **50% quit within six months**
- 75% report loyalty to employer; only **54% feel employer is loyal back**
- **31% likely to change jobs within a year**

## 2019 Annual Survey

### Key Statistics
- **40% of employers planned to hire full-time, permanent employees** in 2019
- 47% planned to recruit part-time workers
- **50% of HR managers said they have open positions they cannot find qualified candidates** for
