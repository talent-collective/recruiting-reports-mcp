# Deloitte Global Human Capital Trends — Annual Series 2015–2020

## 2015 — "Leading in the New World of Work"

**Sample:** 3,300+ respondents in 106 countries

### Culture & Engagement (#1 Priority)
- **87% of HR and business leaders cited lack of employee engagement as a top issue** (up from 79% in 2014)
- **50% called the problem "very important"** (doubled from 26% the prior year)
- Only **13% of global workforce is "highly engaged"** (Gallup data cited)
- Over 50% of workers would not recommend their employer to peers
- Only 7% rate themselves as excellent at measuring, driving, and improving engagement
- Just **12% believe their organizations excel at driving desired culture**
- 22% have poor or no engagement measurement programs
- 66% of HR respondents are updating engagement and retention strategies

### Leadership (#2 Priority)
- **86% cited leadership gaps as a top issue**

### Workplace Complexity
- 66% believe their employees are "overwhelmed" by today's work environment
- 74% cite workplace complexity as a significant problem

### Talent Analytics
- 75% cited talent analytics as an important issue
- Only **8% believe their organization is "strong" in talent analytics**

### Top 10 Trends Ranked
Culture and engagement, leadership, learning and development, reinventing HR, workforce on demand, performance management, simplification of work, machine/people relationship, people analytics, people data

## 2016 — "The New Organization: Different by Design"

**Sample:** 7,000+ responses from 130+ countries (4th annual report)

### Organizational Design (#1 Priority)
- **92% of survey participants rating organizational redesign as a critical priority**
- Nearly half: 39% are in middle of restructuring; 6% planning one

### Leadership Gap
- More than **56% say their companies are not ready to meet leadership needs**
- Only **7% have accelerated millennials into leadership programs**

### Learning Technology Adoption (Year-over-Year)
- MOOC integration rose from 30% to 43%
- Advanced video usage tripled from 5% to 15%

### People Analytics Progress
- Correlation of business impact to HR programs: increased from 38% (2015) to **51% (2016)**
- Predictive workforce analytics usage rose from 29% to **44%**

### Digital HR
- 42% adapting HR systems for mobile delivery
- 59% developing mobile apps integrating back-office systems

### Contingent Workforce
- Only 19% fully understand contingent labor laws
- Only 11% have complete management processes for contingent workers

### Top Priorities Ranked
Organizational design (92%), leadership (89%), culture (86%), engagement (85%), learning (84%), design thinking (79%), people analytics (77%), digital HR (74%), gig economy management (71%)

## 2017 — "Rewriting the Rules for the Digital Age"

**Sample:** 10,000+ business and HR leaders in 140 countries

### Key Statistics
- **92% of executives see a need to redesign the organization** itself
- Organizational design rose to top priority as companies sought to become more agile
- Shift toward "network of teams" model from traditional functional structures
- **73% of respondents said their #1 driver at work is doing a job that has meaning and purpose**
- Only 3% said pay was the top driver

### Major Shift
- Performance management reinvention: companies moving away from annual reviews to continuous feedback models

---

## 2018 — "The Rise of the Social Enterprise"

**Sample:** 11,000+ business and HR leaders globally

### Social Enterprise Theme
- Business represents a new kind of social enterprise, accountable to both shareholders and society
- 52% of global population trusts business "to do what is right" (Edelman Trust Barometer cited)

### Workforce Composition (Projected by 2020)
- 37% expected rise in contractors
- 33% forecasted increase in freelancers
- 28% expected growth in gig workers
- Only **42% of organizations primarily made up of salaried employees**

### Rewards Programs
- Only **8% report their rewards program is "very effective"** at creating personalized, flexible solutions

### Career Management
- **59% rate their organizations as not effective or only somewhat effective** at enabling employee career self-management

### Millennials
- **86% believe business success should be measured beyond financial performance**

### C-Suite Collaboration
- Companies where C-suite executives "regularly collaborate on long-term interdependent work" are **one-third more likely to expect 10%+ annual growth**

### Future Skills Demand (Survey Predictions)
- 63% predicted demand for complex problem-solving
- 55% cited cognitive abilities
- 52% identified social skills

## 2019 — "Leading the Social Enterprise: Reinvent with a Human Focus"

**Sample:** 9,400+ business and HR leaders across 119 countries

### Key Statistics
- Only **6% believe they have best-in-class recruiting processes and technology**
- 70% of respondents cited recruitment as an important issue
- 54% claimed to have increased investment in reskilling their workforce by at least 6%
- **81% rated "ability to lead through more complexity and ambiguity" as top new leadership requirement**

---

## 2020 — "The Social Enterprise at Work: Paradox as a Path Forward"

**Data:** Pre-COVID-19 data with COVID overlay analysis

### Key Statistics
- **97% of respondents stated they need additional information on some aspect of their workforce**
- Only **11% of organizations can produce workforce information in real time**

### Four Key Trends
1. Emergence of employee experience as a business driver
2. Adolescence of people analytics
3. Rediscovery of internal recruiting
4. Rise of multigenerational workforce
