# SHRM: 2026 Recruiting Executives — Priorities and Perspectives

## Key Takeaways

- Top priorities: recruiting critical talent (31%), sourcing (22%), recruiting strategy (15%)
- Critical-talent strategy development held steady at 63% — the most stable priority year-over-year
- Sourcing strategy focus jumped from 23% → 34%; job board reliance jumped from 6% → 25%
- Labor market competition (46%) and talent shortages (42%) are the top macro challenges
- Midlevel nonmanagerial roles are the hardest and most needed to fill (66% of HR pros say so)
- 92% of recruiting executives expect generative AI for job description creation to become more prevalent
- I&D in recruiting is declining: only 5% expect it to become more prevalent; 57% expect less/much less prevalent

---

## Introduction

The role of recruiting has transformed from a support function to a strategic driver of business success. Recruiting leaders now set direction for how orgs compete for talent, manage labor shortages, balance wage pressures, and anticipate candidate expectations and technological disruption.

SHRM research objectives:
1. Understand recruiting executives' priorities for the year ahead
2. Explore challenges — market, organizational, and personal
3. Determine perspectives on emerging recruiting trends (especially AI)

## 2026 Recruiting Executives' Priorities

### Top Priority Areas (overall)

- **31%** — Recruiting critical talent
- **22%** — Sourcing
- **15%** — Recruiting strategy
- Over half of executives identified one of these three as their top priority

### Critical Talent Focus Areas

Priorities for recruiting critical talent remained consistent year-over-year:
- Developing a critical-talent recruiting strategy: **63%** (vs. 64% in 2025 — essentially unchanged)
- Specialized recruiting strategies: minor fluctuation
- Critical-skills needs assessment: minor fluctuation
- Third-party partnerships: minor fluctuation
- Building internal talent marketplaces: minor fluctuation

Stability here suggests ongoing organizational alignment in how companies approach securing top talent.

### Sourcing Focus Areas (notable year-over-year shifts)

| Focus Area | 2025 | 2026 | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Creating a sourcing strategy | 23% | **34%** | +11 pts |
| Reliance on job boards/career websites | 6% | **25%** | +19 pts |
| Active sourcing (candidates actively job hunting) | 37% | **28%** | -9 pts |
| Passive sourcing (candidates not actively looking) | 15% | **5%** | -10 pts |

**Interpretation:** Executives are shifting from proactive outbound sourcing toward structured strategy development and more traditional channels. Less emphasis on passive candidate hunting.

### Recruiting Strategy Focus Areas (notable year-over-year shifts)

| Focus Area | 2025 | 2026 | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Recruiting function strategy | 30% | **42%** | +12 pts |
| Managing recruiting initiatives | 27% | **31%** | +4 pts |
| Hiring manager/stakeholder engagement | 22% | **16%** | -6 pts |
| Recruiting analytics and metrics | 12% | **11%** | -1 pt |
| Recruiter talent development | 10% | **0%** | -10 pts (fell off entirely) |

**Interpretation:** Shift toward high-level planning over day-to-day operations. Recruiter development dropped completely off the radar — notable given the AI upskilling challenges cited separately.

## 2026 Recruiting Executives' Challenges

### Macroeconomic / Market Challenges

| Challenge | % Citing | % Greatest Challenge |
|---|---|---|
| Labor market competition | 46% | 16% |
| Talent shortages | 42% | 14% |
| Wage inflation | 42% | 13% |
| Skills shortages | 39% | — |
| Remote and hybrid work | 34% | — |
| Demanding candidate expectations | 33% | — |
| Technological advancements | 22% | — |

**Labor market context (SHRM March 2026 Monthly Market Review):** Total nonfarm employment declined 92,000 in February 2026. Reductions in 12 of 21 major industrial sectors, including healthcare and social assistance (typically a driver of job gains). Modest gains in professional, scientific, and technical services only.

### Hiring Needs by Job Level

| Level | % Rating as Greatest Hiring Need |
|---|---|
| Midlevel nonmanagerial | **41%** |
| Entry-level | **37%** |
| Midlevel managerial | 11% |
| Senior/executive | 11% |

Consistent with SHRM's 2026 Talent Trends (broader HR pro survey):
- **66%** cited midlevel nonmanagerial as most difficult to fill
- **50%** said entry-level
- **37%** said midlevel managerial
- **19%** said senior/executive

### Organizational Challenges

| Challenge | % Citing | % Greatest Challenge |
|---|---|---|
| Lack of qualified candidates | 49% | 20% |
| Sourcing difficult-to-fill positions | 41% | 16% |
| Offering competitive salaries/wages | 41% | 14% |
| Candidate ghosting | 29% | — |
| Time filtering irrelevant applications | 28% | — |

Additional context from SHRM 2026 Talent Trends: **77% of HR professionals** report difficulties hiring for roles requiring new skills.

### Recruiting Function Challenges

Note: CHROs also identified budget constraints as their top function challenge in SHRM's 2026 CHRO Priorities and Perspectives report — alignment across HR leadership levels.

### Personal Challenges (for recruiting executives themselves)

## 2026 Recruiting Trends and Predictions

### AI in Recruiting Workflows (% expecting more/much more prevalent)

| AI Application | % Expecting More Prevalent |
|---|---|
| Generative AI to create job descriptions/content | **92%** (40% say "much more prevalent") |
| Broader automation of general recruiting processes | **87%** |
| Chatbots and automated resume screening | **85%** |
| AI for recruiting metrics and predictive analytics | **85%** |
| AI phasing out repetitive work | **81%** |
| AI skills as a job qualification on open roles | **78%** |
| AI-powered bias monitoring tools | **68%** |

Note: SHRM's State of AI in HR 2026 confirms job description drafting/refinement is already the most prevalent AI use case across HR functions.

### AI and the Candidate Experience (% expecting more/much more prevalent)

| Application | % Expecting More Prevalent |
|---|---|
| Candidates using AI to apply for jobs | **85%** |
| Candidates using AI to interview | **74%** |
| AI tools providing real-time feedback during application/interview | **72%** |
| Increased transparency via automated updates/notifications | **69%** |
| AI chatbots for personalized candidate experiences | **67%** |
| VR/AR for candidate assessments and interviews | **40%** |

### Core Recruiting Strategy Trends (% expecting more prevalent)

| Trend | % Expecting More Prevalent |
|---|---|
| Mobile-friendly recruiting strategy | **73%** |
| Pay transparency (salary ranges on job postings) | **71%** |
| Streamlined application process | **71%** |
| Social media in recruiting | **64%** |
| Promoting org culture in job descriptions/ads | **64%** |
| Skills-first qualifications over traditional credentials | **59%** |
| I&D focus in recruiting | **5%** more prevalent; **57%** expect less/much less |

**On I&D:** The 57% expecting less/much less prevalent signals a significant re-evaluation of how I&D initiatives are integrated into talent acquisition — one of the most striking findings in the report.

### Skill Alignment Challenges (% expecting more prevalent)

| Challenge | % Expecting More Prevalent |
|---|---|
| Candidates applying for significantly underqualified roles | **53%** |
| Overall misalignment between requirements and applicant skills | **45%** |
| Candidates applying for significantly overqualified roles | **43%** |

## What's Next — Key Themes

### 1. Aggressive AI Adoption to Revolutionize Recruiting Workflows
92% expect generative AI for JD creation to grow; 87% expect broader process automation; 85% foresee AI driving metrics and predictive analytics. Leaders are reimagining operations to reduce admin burden and focus on high-value human interactions.

### 2. Targeted Sourcing Strategies for Critical Talent Remain the Top Priority
63% prioritizing critical-talent recruiting strategy; sourcing strategy focus jumped from 23% → 34%. Shift from reactive hiring to proactive pipeline building aligned with long-term business goals.

### 3. Candidate Experience Evolves Through Transparency and Accessibility
73% expect mobile-friendly recruiting to grow; 71% expect more salary transparency; 71% expect streamlined applications. Companies are removing barriers to create a smoother, more engaging path for candidates.
