# Ashby — Candidate Experience Surveys Report

## Response Rate Benchmarks

- Overall average response rate: **17.8%** (range: 16–19%)
- 94% of responses arrive within **9 days** of delivery

**By survey length:**
| Length | Response Rate |
|--------|--------------|
| 1–5 questions | 20.1% |
| 6–10 questions | 20.1% |
| 11+ questions | 18.2% |

**By anonymity setting:**
| Setting | Response Rate |
|---------|--------------|
| Do Not Allow Anonymous | **20.6%** (highest) |
| Allow Anonymous | 17.7% |
| Enforce Anonymous | 15.3% (lowest) |

**By hiring stage:**
- Lowest: candidates rejected at initial screen
- Highest: hired candidates

---

## Candidate NPS Benchmarks

- **Average Candidate NPS: 37**

**By outcome:**
| Outcome | NPS |
|---------|-----|
| Hired candidates | 85 |
| Offer recipients | 62 |
| Active candidates (2+ interview stages) | 28 |

**Time-in-process NPS impact:**
- 15–59 days: minimal variance
- 60+ days: significant decline
  - Rejected candidates: **54.9% NPS drop**
  - Hired candidates: **16.5% NPS drop**

## Customer Case Studies

**Notion:**
- Customized questions tied to recruiter clarity, timeliness, interviewer preparation
- Free-text prompts for qualitative insights
- 3-day delay before survey delivery post-rejection

**Gorgias:**
- 20% of project time dedicated to candidate experience ownership
- Segments surveys by hiring stage
- Monitors NPS by department, outcome, and individual recruiter

**M-KOPA:**
- Rejects anonymous submission to enable contextual analysis
- Prioritizes recruiter accountability

---

## Key Conclusions

- Non-anonymous surveys yield highest response rates
- 6–10 question surveys are the sweet spot (high response, sufficient data)
- Candidate NPS drops sharply after 60 days in process — speed matters for experience
- Hired candidate NPS (85) vs. rejected active candidate NPS (28) — a 57-point gap
