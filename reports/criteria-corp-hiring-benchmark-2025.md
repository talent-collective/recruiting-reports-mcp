# Criteria Corp — Hiring Benchmark Report 2024-2025
**Source:** Criteria Corp
**Published:** 2024 (7th annual edition)
**Data:** 425+ hiring professionals surveyed globally, primarily US, Australia, and Canada (July 2024)
**URL:** https://www.criteriacorp.com/research/2024-2025-hiring-benchmark-report

## Methodology
Seventh annual survey of 425+ hiring professionals across organizations of all sizes, collected July 2024. Primarily US, Australia, and Canada respondents.

## Hiring Plans
- Over 50% of hiring professionals plan to increase hiring in the next year
- Small businesses show particularly strong growth expectations

## Candidate Ghosting Gap
- Only 20% of hiring professionals acknowledge ghosting candidates
- 38% of candidates report being ghosted within the past year
- Significant perception gap between recruiter and candidate experience

## Talent Shortage
- 70% of hiring professionals believe a talent shortage exists
- Hardest-to-find skills: industry-specific knowledge and soft skills

## Skills-Based Hiring Adoption
- Over 50% of companies adopting skills-based hiring approaches
- 74% evaluate skills using new methods
- 68% rewrote job descriptions to emphasize skills over credentials
- 20% removed college degree requirements

## AI in Hiring
- 25% of hiring professionals use AI for hiring or talent management (double the previous year)
- Only 9% trust AI more than traditional recruitment methods
- AI adoption accelerating but trust remains cautious

## Soft Skills Priorities
- 75% identify emotional intelligence as the most important soft skill
- Followed by: analytical thinking, flexibility/agility, and resilience

## Gen Z Workforce Readiness
- Only 8% of hiring professionals believe Gen Z is fully prepared entering the workforce
- Employers cite challenges with teamwork and professional readiness
