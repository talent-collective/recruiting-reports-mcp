# iCIMS: 2026 Workforce Reports (Monthly Series)

## January 2026 Workforce Report (Primary Annual Edition)

### Labor Market Data (December 2025 / Year-End)
- Job openings increased **6% month-over-month** in December
- Job openings up **8% year-over-year**
- Growth driven by: manufacturing (+2%), healthcare (+8%), transportation (+12%)
- Applications up **7% year-over-year**
- U.S. unemployment rate reached its highest level in 4 years in November
- Internal applications up **8% year-over-year** (interest in internal mobility remains strong)
- Job openings and job seeker activity remained elevated into December — defying the typical seasonal slowdown

### Job Seeker Sentiment (survey of 1,000 U.S. job seekers)
- **58%** say they are not concerned about being laid off
- **45%** plan to look for a new role in 2026 (up from 42% last year)
- Workers entering 2026 with "cautious optimism"

### Talent Acquisition Leader Priorities (survey of 250 TA leaders)
- **84%** say improving recruiter efficiency through AI is a top priority
- **87%** flag legal compliance as a concern around AI
- **66%** worry about candidate trust with AI
- **65%** concerned about potential bias from AI
- Leaders want greater control, defensibility, and operational clarity in hiring in 2026

### Expert Analyst Outlook
Experts from Aptitude Research, Kyle & Co., Lighthouse Research & Advisory, and Mercer contributed:
- Organizations pursuing greater control and defensibility in hiring processes
- Balance between AI efficiency and human oversight is the central tension
