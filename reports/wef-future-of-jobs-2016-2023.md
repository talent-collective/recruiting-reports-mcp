# World Economic Forum — Future of Jobs Reports 2016–2023

**Source:** World Economic Forum (weforum.org/publications)
**Data:** Surveys of senior executives, chief human resources officers, and global company representatives
**Period:** 2016, 2018, 2020, 2023 (published every 2–3 years)
**Best for:** Long-range workforce transformation forecasts, jobs displaced vs. created projections, top skills demand trends, reskilling priorities, automation impact

---

## 2016 Report — "The Future of Jobs"

**Published:** January 2016
**Sample:** 300+ global companies representing 13 million+ employees across 9 broad industry sectors in 15 major developed and emerging economies

### Job Impact (2015–2020 Horizon)
- **5.1 million net jobs lost** to disruptive labor market changes over 2015–2020
- Total job loss projected: **7.1 million**
- New jobs created: **~2 million** (in more specialized roles)

### Key Drivers
- Fourth Industrial Revolution: AI, machine learning, robotics, nanotechnology, 3D printing, genetics/biotechnology
- Socioeconomic drivers: growing middle class, urbanization, aging populations
- Short-term technology drivers (2015–2017): mobile internet, cloud technology, cheaper computing power, big data
- "Routine white-collar office functions" at highest risk of displacement

### Top 10 Skills in Demand (2015–2020)
1. Complex problem-solving
2. Critical thinking
3. Creativity
4. People management
5. Coordinating with others
6. Emotional intelligence
7. Judgment and decision-making
8. Service orientation
9. Negotiation
10. Cognitive flexibility

## 2018 Report — "The Future of Jobs 2018"

**Published:** September 2018
**Sample:** 300+ global companies representing 15+ million employees; 20 developed and emerging economies representing ~70% of global economy

### Job Impact (by 2022)
- **75 million jobs displaced** by 2022 due to automation
- **133 million new roles** may emerge (net gain of **58 million jobs** by 2022)
- Machines expected to perform **42% of all current workplace tasks** by 2022 (vs. 29% at time of survey)
- Potential for full-time permanent employment to fall in favor of temporary workers, freelancers, and contractors

### Key Skills for 2022
1. Analytical thinking and innovation
2. Active learning and learning strategies
3. Creativity, originality, and initiative
4. Technology design and programming
5. Critical thinking and analysis

### Notable Shift from 2016
- 2016 forecast: net loss of 5.1M jobs
- 2018 forecast: net gain of 58M jobs — reflecting recognition of new role creation

## 2020 Report — "The Future of Jobs 2020"

**Published:** October 2020
**Context:** COVID-19 pandemic impact on workforce transformation — "double-disruption" scenario

### Job Impact (by 2025)
- **85 million jobs displaced** in the next five years by automation
- **97 million new jobs created** — net addition of **12 million jobs**

### "Double-Disruption" Scenario
- Automation combined with COVID-19 recession creating unprecedented workforce disruption

### Workforce Transformation
- **43% of businesses plan to reduce workforce** due to technology integration
- 41% plan to expand use of contractors for task-specialized work
- 34% plan to expand workforce due to technology integration

### Remote Work
- **84% of employers set to rapidly digitalize work processes**
- **44% of their workforce** has potential to operate remotely

### Reskilling
- **Half of all employees will need reskilling in the next five years**

### Disproportionate Impact
- Lower-wage workers, women, and younger workers most affected in first phase of economic contraction

## 2023 Report — "The Future of Jobs 2023"

**Published:** April/May 2023
**Sample:** 803 companies representing 673 million jobs across 27 industry clusters and 45 economies

### Job Impact (2023–2027)
- **83 million jobs eliminated** in 2023–2027 period
- **69 million new jobs** created in same period
- **Net decrease: 14 million jobs** (2% of current employment)
- **23% of jobs expected to change** by 2027

### Growth and Decline Rates
- Job growth: 10.2% of current employment
- Job decline: 12.3% of current employment

### Key Growth Drivers
- Green transition, ESG standards, and localization of supply chains (leading job growth)
- Economic challenges (inflation, slower growth, supply shortages) posing greatest threat

### AI Impact
- AI adopted by **nearly 75% of surveyed companies**
- **50% of organizations expect AI to create job growth**
- **25% expect AI to create job losses**

### Fastest Growing Roles
1. AI and Machine Learning Specialists
2. Sustainability Specialists
3. Business Intelligence Analysts
4. Information Security Analysts

### Fastest Declining Roles (driven by technology and digitalization)
- Bank Tellers and Related Clerks
- Cashiers and Ticket Clerks
- Data Entry Clerks
- Administrative and Executive Secretaries

### Fastest Growing by Absolute Numbers
Education, agriculture, and digital commerce

### Reskilling
- Average companies estimate **40% of workers will require reskilling** of 6 months or less
- **94% of business leaders expect employees to pick up new skills on the job** (vs. 65% in 2018)
