# Mercer — Global Talent Trends 2024-2025

## Methodology
Annual survey of approximately 12,000 respondents including business executives, HR leaders, employees, and investors. 16 geographies and 16 industries represented. Supplemented by a Pulse survey of 1,800 HR leaders across 24 key markets.

## Four Major Workforce Shifts

1. **Reinvent for a human advantage** — Organizations redesigning work to integrate human capability with automation and AI while developing future-fit skills
2. **Leap forward with insight** — Leaders emphasizing talent intelligence and workforce data to address AI skills gaps
3. **Recalibrate the value exchange** — Companies reassessing employee value propositions around experience, communication, and engagement
4. **Unleash a new HR era** — HR functions evolving toward more strategic roles in shaping work and organizational effectiveness

## Key Findings

- 1 in 5 organizations report difficulty filling open positions has significantly impacted business performance (higher recruitment costs, project delays)
- Only 30% of organizations are regularly using generative AI (significant adoption gap)
- High-growth firms are significantly ahead across all four talent trends and more likely to be doubling down on AI investments
- Transition toward skills-based talent and reward practices gaining traction

## Recruiting and Talent Acquisition Implications
- Talent shortage impact is measurable: delayed projects and higher recruitment costs are the primary business consequences
- Skills-based hiring alignment with broader workforce skills strategy is accelerating
- AI adoption gap between leaders and laggards is widening
