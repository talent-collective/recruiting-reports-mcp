# Deloitte Global Human Capital Trends — Annual Series 2021–2024

**Source:** Deloitte Insights (deloitte.com/us/en/insights)
**Data:** Annual global surveys of business and HR leaders
**Period:** 2021–2024
**Best for:** Remote work impact, workforce adaptability, worker-employer relationship evolution, human sustainability gap, burnout rates, AI and technology integration, hybrid work challenges

---

## 2021 — "The Social Enterprise in a World Disrupted"

**Published:** February 2021
**Sample:** 6,000 professionals across every industry, sector, and region; 99 countries; including 3,630 senior executives

### Work Reimagination
- **61% of leaders are reimagining work** — more than double the 29% doing so pre-pandemic
- 54% of European senior executives intend to focus on reimagining work in next 1–3 years (vs. 28% pre-COVID)

### Remote Work & Wellbeing
- **7 in 10 executives observed the shift to remote work had a positive influence on wellbeing**
- **90% agreed that today's remote work practices will be sustainable**
- 60% positive wellbeing impact for businesses using remote/virtual practices

### Organizational Preparedness
- **17% of organizations will now prepare for unprecedented events** (up from 6% pre-pandemic)
- 47% plan to prepare for multiple future scenarios (23% increase from pre-pandemic)

### Workforce Adaptability
- **72% say "ability of their people to adapt, reskill, and assume new roles"** is the most important or second most important factor in navigating disruptions
- Only **17% say their organization is "very ready" to adapt and reskill workers**

### Worker-Employer Dynamics
- **86% believe workers will gain greater independence** relative to employers in the future
- 63% of workers think their relationship with employers will either become stronger or stay the same

### Historic Shift
- For the first time, **business respondents (56%) outnumbered HR respondents (44%)** — underscoring growing importance of human capital

## 2022 — "Evolving Together: The Worker-Employer Relationship"

**Sample:** 3,600+ executives in 96 countries; 1,200+ C-suite executives and board members; 233 CEOs

### Key Findings
- Theme: Pandemic and societal pressures accelerated evolution of worker-employer relationship beyond anyone's anticipation
- Two most influential factors for future worker-employer relationships: talent supply and government impact
- Organizations need to move beyond reactive responses to develop sustainable, human-centric workforce strategies
- Business respondents again outnumbered HR respondents (56% vs. 44%)

## 2023 — "New Fundamentals for a Boundaryless World"

**Sample:** 10,000+ business and HR leaders; 105 countries; every industry

### Workplace Model
- **87% of business leaders believe developing the right workplace model is important or very important**
- Only **24% feel their organization is very ready** to address this trend
- 78% are trying to create a future workplace by redesigning existing business processes or reimagining work

### Remote Work Flexibility
- **Two-thirds (64%) of workers globally say they would consider looking for a new job** if employer wanted them back full-time in office
- Only 15% cite the way work is designed as among the most important attributes for the future workplace

### Technology & Performance
- More than **90% believe using technology to improve work outcomes and team performance is important**
- Only **22% believe their organizations are very ready** to use technology to improve work outcomes

### Key Theme
Work isn't defined by jobs, the workplace isn't a specific place, and many workers aren't traditional employees — a "boundaryless world" of work

## 2024 — "Thriving in a Boundaryless World"

**Published:** January/February 2024
**Sample:** 14,000 respondents across 95 countries; supplemented with worker and executive-specific surveys

### Human Sustainability Gap
- **89% of executives say their organization advances human sustainability**
- Only **41% of workers agree with this assessment**
- Only **43% of workers report organizations left them better off than when hired**

### Worker Challenges
- **53% of workers say they worry most about increasing work stress leading to worse mental health**
- **48% of workers and 53% of managers admit they are burned out at work**
- Nearly half of millennial and Gen Z workers report feeling stressed all or most of the time

### Hybrid Work
- **85% of leaders say the shift to hybrid work has made it challenging to gauge productivity**

### AI and Technology
- Only **10% of organizations say they are leading** in advancing human sustainability
- Organizations making meaningful progress on key issues are **nearly twice as likely to achieve desired outcomes**
- Only **9% making meaningful progress** toward balancing human imagination with technology
- **73% say ensuring human imagination keeps pace with tech is important**

### Trust
- **35% more likely to trust business** when confident data is used responsibly
- Only **37% say organizations use data in a "highly responsible" way**

### Top C-Suite Challenges
- **31% of C-suite identifies HR transformation as among three most difficult trends**
- 71% prioritize team-level culture and diversity cultivation
