# Talent Board Candidate Experience (CandE) Research — Annual Series 2015–2024

**Source:** Talent Board (thetalentboard.org) — CandE Awards program
**Data:** Annual benchmark research across participating employers and job seekers; continuous since 2011
**Period:** 2015–2024
**Best for:** Candidate experience benchmarks, resentment rates, application length, communication gaps, DEI in candidate experience, AI technology adoption for screening, employer brand impact of candidate experience

---

## Program Overview

The Talent Board Candidate Experience (CandE) program is the longest-running annual benchmark research on recruiting and candidate experience. Since 2011, the program has gathered data from 1,350+ employers and 1.4 million+ job seekers covering pre-application through onboarding stages.

**Key metric tracked:** "Candidate resentment" — the percentage of candidates who stop engaging with a brand as a consumer due to a poor recruiting experience.

---

## 2015 Research

**Program:** Annual benchmark research since 2011
- Covers pre-application through onboarding stages
- Measures candidate resentment as proxy for business impact of poor experience
- Data from 1,350+ employers and 1.4 million job seekers since launch

## 2016 Research

**Sample:** 200+ companies; 180,000+ candidates (EMEA: 152 participating companies; 25,000+ candidates)
**Published:** February 2017

### Key Statistics
- **66% of candidates share negative recruiting experiences** with their inner circles
- **34% of candidates will share negative experiences publicly** (LinkedIn, Glassdoor, etc.)
- **26% of candidates** reported their application took 15 minutes or less
- **79% of employers offered mobile-enabled applications** (up from 67%)
- 64% of candidates who rated their experience **five stars said they would increase their business relationship** with the employer

---

## 2017 Research

**Sample:** 200+ companies; 180,000+ candidates

### Key Statistics
- 20% of candidates reported applications taking **15 minutes or less** (down from 26% in 2016)
- **Mobile-enabled applications jumped from 79% to 91%** year-over-year
- Talent Board surveys more than 180,000 candidates applying to roles at 200 companies annually

## 2018 Research

**Sample:** 200 participating companies; 130,000 job seekers providing benchmark survey feedback

### Key Statistics
- **64% of North American employers describe their overall candidate experience as leading or competing**
- Nearly 25% describe their experience as improving
- Less than 2% describe their experience as lagging
- **92% overall of participants were rejected, hadn't heard back, or did not know of any offer** — these candidates have a greater aggregate impact on brand than those who are hired

### CandE Award Winners vs. All Others
- 26% more likely to align recruiter performance with candidate experience
- **68% more likely to use artificial intelligence in recruiting**

---

## 2020 Research (COVID-19 Impact)

**Sample:** 133 organizations in North America; 152,000 candidates

### Key Statistics & Findings
- **Pre-COVID candidate resentment had been trending upward; completely reversed in 2020**
- Candidate resentment decreased as employers communicated transparently about COVID-19 challenges
- Candidates adopted a more forgiving attitude due to empathetic communication about hiring freezes and process changes
- Employers who communicated about pandemic challenges saw improved candidate willingness to maintain brand relationship

---

## 2021 Research

**Sample:** 150+ companies; nearly 200,000 job candidates worldwide

### Key Statistics
- **North American candidate resentment rose from 8% (2020) to 14% (2021)** — a **75% spike**
- The **largest single-year increase** since Talent Board began research a decade ago
- 43% more candidates from CandE-winning companies were asked for feedback at end of interview stage vs. all other employers
- Women and people of color rated 2021 candidate experiences **more positively** than older, male, or white/Caucasian candidates

## 2022 Research

**Sample:** 150+ companies; nearly 200,000 candidates globally
**Published:** Early 2023

### Candidate Resentment (by Region)
| Region | Change |
|--------|--------|
| EMEA | +10% |
| APAC | +17% |
| Latin America | +100% |
| North America | -14% (but still 12% overall) |

### Communication Failures
- **34% of candidates waited one to two months or longer for post-application updates** — a **48% increase** from 2021 data

### Top Recruiting Priorities for 2023
1. Onboarding: 40%
2. Candidate experience: 38%
3. Diversity/inclusion (tied): 37%
4. Employee referrals (tied): 37%

### Company Values
- Company values emerged as the **top research content candidates reviewed before applying** — described as "never been more valuable" in benchmark history

### AI Technology Investments Planned
- **79% plan bias-removal tech for screening**
- 72% plan employee job-change prediction tools
- 57% plan job performance prediction technology

## 2024 Research

### Key Statistics
- CandE Winners had **36% higher assessment perception of fairness** vs. all other companies combined
- CandE Winners had **21% higher interview perception of fairness**
- **Highest-rated companies are dispositioning candidates within 3–5 days after application**
