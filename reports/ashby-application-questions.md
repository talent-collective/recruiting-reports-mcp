# Ashby — Are Jobs Asking More Application Questions?

## Key Finding

**Application questions per job posting have nearly doubled over 4 years**

---

## Long-Form Questions

- **46.8%** of job applications in 2024 included at least one long-form question (up significantly over period)
- Prompted Ashby to develop AI-assisted filtering features

---

## Completion Behavior

- Despite increased questions, **average rate of questions answered is relatively stable**
- Jobs with more questions tend to include more optional questions
- Applicants continue submitting even with longer forms
- Data reflects only submitted applications — doesn't capture abandonment during completion

---

## Perspectives in the Market

**Candidate view:**
- Some see extensive questions as barriers to applying
- Others view them as opportunities to stand out

**Recruiter view:**
- Some value additional vetting signal
- Others struggle with volume of AI-generated responses to long-form questions

---

## Key Conclusions

- Application question volume has roughly doubled since 2021, tracking alongside the surge in total application volume
- Nearly half of 2024 job applications included long-form questions
- Completion rates have held steady despite longer forms — candidates who apply still answer questions
- AI-generated responses to application questions are an emerging concern for recruiters
