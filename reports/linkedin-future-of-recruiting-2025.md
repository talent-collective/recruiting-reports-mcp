# LinkedIn Future of Recruiting 2025

## Methodology
Survey of 1,271 recruiting professionals in management roles or higher across 23 countries in 4 languages, conducted September 2024. Supplemented with LinkedIn platform data from 1B+ members across 200+ countries using 12-month rolling comparisons.

## AI in Recruiting

### Adoption
- 37% of organizations actively integrating or experimenting with gen AI (up from 27% year-over-year)
  - 11% actively integrating
  - 26% experimenting
  - 31% exploring
  - 32% not currently using
- 73% of TA professionals agree AI will change how organizations hire
- 2.3x growth in TA professionals using LinkedIn Learning for AI skills (Oct 2023 to Sept 2024)

### Efficiency
- 20% weekly time savings for teams experimenting with or integrating gen AI (approximately one full workday per week)

### Top Benefits of Gen AI
1. Improved efficiency
2. Better candidate experience
3. Enhanced candidate quality
4. Reduced time-to-hire

### Challenges to Adoption
- Data privacy concerns
- Legal compliance uncertainty
- Lack of clarity on implementation
- Bias mitigation

### Time Freed by AI
- 35% of TA pros use freed time for candidate screening
- 26% focus on skill assessments

## Quality of Hire

- 89% of TA professionals agree measuring quality of hire is increasingly important
- 25% feel highly confident in their organization's ability to measure it effectively
- 61% believe AI can improve quality of hire measurement
- +9% improvement in quality hires for companies using AI-Assisted Messaging most vs. least

### How Quality of Hire Is Measured
- Job performance ratings: 66% of TA pros
- New hire retention: 60%
- Hiring manager satisfaction: 44%

## Skills-Based Hiring

- 93% of TA professionals believe accurately assessing candidate skills is crucial for quality hiring
- +12% quality hire improvement for companies with most skills-based searches
- Relationship development demand increased 54x year-over-year in paid recruiting job posts

### Degree Requirement Trends
- 2023: 26% of paid LinkedIn job posts required no degree
- 2020: 22% of paid LinkedIn job posts required no degree
- Growth of +4 percentage points (16% increase) over 3 years
