# Ashby — What Is a Recruiter's Capacity?

**Source:** ashbyhq.com/talent-trends-report  
**Data:** 26M+ interactions; 100K jobs  
**Period:** November 2024 – August 2025  
**Best for:** Recruiter and coordinator capacity benchmarks, company-size comparison, peak load patterns

---

## Capacity Benchmarks (90th Percentile = Peak Capacity)

| Role | Jobs/Week at Peak |
|------|-----------------|
| Recruiting Coordinators | **27 jobs/week** |
| Recruiters & Sourcers | **14 jobs/week** |

---

## Company Size Effect

- Recruiting Coordinators at enterprise organizations handle **3x more jobs/week** than those at <100-employee companies

---

## Nine-Month Trend

- Busiest recruiters: increased from **~22 jobs/week** (start of period) to **~27 jobs/week** (end of period)
- Efficiency has improved over the nine months analyzed

---

## Participation Rates

- **~33%** of talent professionals reached 90th percentile capacity at least once annually
- **>50%** of organizations had someone hit peak capacity levels

---

## Seasonal Pattern

- Capacity remains steady year-round
- **Exception:** Holiday periods show reduced load
- High-demand weeks are temporary, not sustained for most professionals

---

## Methodology

- Interactions tracked: application reviews, job-related emails, interviews (accepted/tentative), scheduled interviews
- Capacity defined as actively managed jobs/week at 90th percentile

---

## Case Study: Mapbox

- Post-$280M Series E: 150% interview volume increase
- Deployed 4 coordinators across regions
- Adopted advanced scheduling technology to manage expanded capacity

---

## Key Conclusions

- Peak capacity (~27 jobs/week for coordinators, ~14 for recruiters) is a meaningful planning benchmark
- More than half of orgs will have someone hit peak capacity in a given year
- Efficiency improved over the 9-month period — technology likely a contributing factor
