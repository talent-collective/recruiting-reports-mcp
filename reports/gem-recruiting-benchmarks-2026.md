# Gem — 2026 Recruiting Benchmarks Report

## Market Conditions

- Hiring grew **8.3% YoY** — but volumes remain **30% below 2021 levels** (this is the new baseline)
- Only **0.5% of applicants** receive a job offer

---

## Recruiter Workload (vs. 2021)

| Metric | Change vs. 2021 |
|--------|----------------|
| Applications per recruiter | **+93%** |
| Open roles managed | **+40%** |
| Team size | **-14%** |
| Hires per recruiter | **-43%** |

Teams are doing dramatically more with fewer people — and converting less.

---

## Offer Acceptance

- **OAR: 82%** — highest since 2021

---

## Interview Volume

- Interviews per hire: **up 33%** overall since 2021
- Technical roles: **35–36 interviews per hire**
- Technical roles: **~26 interviewer hours** per role

---

## Sourcing Channel Performance

- Sourced candidates are **8x more likely to be hired** than other channels
- **46% of sourced hires** come from rediscovered CRM/ATS candidates (up from 26% in 2021)
- Job boards + company marketing = ~90% of applications but only ~50% of hires
- Relationship-driven channels (sourcing, referrals) punch far above their application volume share

---

## Key Conclusions

- Recruiting teams are running harder and hiring less — capacity is the defining constraint of 2026
- Sourcing CRM/ATS re-engagement is the highest-ROI channel, nearly doubling since 2021
- The offer acceptance rate recovery (82%) suggests candidates are more selective but committed once they decide
- Interview inflation (33% more per hire) is a hidden efficiency drag on every recruiting team
