# LinkedIn: 2026 Global Talent Report
**Source:** LinkedIn Talent Solutions (released early 2026)  
**Data:** 1.2B+ LinkedIn members, 12B+ data points

---

## Key Findings

### AI in Recruiting
- **93%** of recruiters plan to increase their use of AI in 2026
- **59%** say AI is already helping them find candidates they wouldn't have found otherwise
- **Two-thirds** of recruiters plan to use AI for pre-screening interviews
- **70%** believe AI pre-screening leads to higher-quality conversations

### Job Seeker Activity
- Weekly job search activity: **78 million unique users** (up 20% from 65M in 2024)
- Steepest growth from users aged 25–34 in emerging markets:
  - India: +41%
  - Brazil: +33%
  - Nigeria: +29%
- **61%** of weekly searchers classified as "passive browsers"
- **52%** of people globally are looking for a new role in 2026

### Hiring Activity
- LinkedIn hiring rate (confirmed hires via members adding new employer) remains below prior years
- Year-over-year decline is slower than 2023, but still below pre-2022 levels
- Higher application volume does not equal higher candidate quality

### Skills-First Hiring
- **75%** of recruiters say skills-based hiring will be their top priority
- **52%** of employers have relaxed educational requirements to focus on skills/experience
- AI often matches on skills, not job titles

### Application Volume
- Between 2023–2025: became significantly easier for candidates to apply (fewer steps, faster applications, widespread one-click/"Easy Apply")
- Surge in application volume has not translated to proportional increase in candidate quality

### Platform Scale
- 1.2 billion users globally
- 65 million decision-makers on the platform
- 6 people hired every minute via LinkedIn
- 89% of recruiters active on the platform
- 10.3% average InMail response rate
- Personal profiles drive 2.75x more impressions than company pages

### Soft Skills and Quality of Hire
- 89% of bad hires lack critical soft skills regardless of technical proficiency
- LinkedIn learning and assessment tools increasingly used to evaluate soft skills pre-offer

### Employer Confidence
- Glassdoor Employee Confidence Index: 46.5% positive 6-month business outlook in December 2025 (down ~5–6 percentage points from low-50s range throughout 2023)
- Lower employer confidence affects employee retention decisions and how talent evaluates current employers
