# Ashby — How Many Hires Come From Inbound?

**Source:** ashbyhq.com/talent-trends-report  
**Data:** ~250,000 hires  
**Period:** January 2021 – June 2025  
**Best for:** Source-of-hire benchmarks, inbound vs. sourcing vs. referral split, company size patterns

---

## Inbound Hiring Trends (2021–2025)

- Inbound accounts for **43–52% of all hires** over the past four years
- Q2 2025: Inbound reached **52%** — highest point in 4 years
- +9 percentage point increase compared to Q4 2022

---

## Source of Hire Breakdown (Q2 2025)

| Source | Share of Hires |
|--------|---------------|
| Inbound | **~52%** |
| Referrals | ~18% |
| Sourced | ~16% |
| Agency | declining |
| Internal | rising (small fraction) |

---

## By Company Size

**Small organizations:** Wide variation ("dumbbell pattern") — some lean heavily inbound, others rely on alternative sources

**Enterprise (500–10,000 employees):**
- Converge toward middle distribution
- Nearly half make majority of hires from inbound
- ~10% hire 70%+ from inbound

---

## Role-Level Patterns

| Role Type | Inbound Share | Sourcing Share |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| Business roles | Higher than average | 18% |
| Tech roles | Lower than average | 24% |
| Executive roles | ~35% inbound | Higher sourcing |

- **Low inbound volume jobs:** sourcing dominates
- **High inbound volume jobs:** inbound hires increase, sourcing share decreases
- **Referrals:** remain consistent at ~17% regardless of inbound volume

---

## Key Conclusions

- Inbound is and remains the dominant source of hire
- Referrals are stable and reliable (~17–18%) regardless of market conditions
- Sourcing is most critical for tech and executive roles where inbound is structurally lower
- The 2025 inbound surge likely driven by post-layoff candidate availability and increased application volume
