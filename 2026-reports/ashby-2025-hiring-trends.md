# Ashby — Did Hiring Pick Up in 2025?

**Source:** ashbyhq.com/talent-trends-report  
**Data:** Fixed cohort of hundreds of companies tracked across 8 consecutive quarters  
**Period:** 2024–2025 (Q1 2024 as baseline)  
**Best for:** 2025 hiring recovery signals, company-size divergence, EMEA vs. North America comparison

---

## Overall Hiring Trend

- Hiring increased in 2025 relative to 2024
- **Every quarter of 2025 showed at least 11% more hiring than Q1 2024 baseline**
- Primary driver: larger organizations (1,001+ employees)

---

## By Company Size

| Segment | 2025 vs. 2024 |
|---------|--------------|
| <100 employees | **Declined** — down up to 25% vs. Q1 2024 baseline in all 4 quarters |
| 101–1,000 employees | Mixed — 250–1,000 range nearly unchanged YoY |
| 1,001+ employees | **Steady expansion** throughout 2024 and 2025 |

---

## By Geography

| Region | 2024→2025 Change |
|--------|----------------|
| North America | More consistent, larger hiring gains |
| EMEA | Only **+2%** growth from 2024 to 2025 |

- Gap between regions widened as company size increased

---

## Additional Context

- Talent teams are **interviewing significantly more candidates per hire**
- Market influenced by AI, interest rates, and regulatory environment
- Hiring remains fundamentally different from the post-2020 boom years

---

## Methodology

- Tracked fixed cohort of hundreds of companies over 8 quarters (Q1 2024 – Q4 2025)
- Q1 2024 used as baseline quarter
- Measured relative change, not point-in-time snapshots
